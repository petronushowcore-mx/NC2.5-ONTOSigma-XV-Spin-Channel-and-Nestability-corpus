## Abstract

This companion carries the formal treatment of the three structural statements of ONTOΣ XV within the categorical apparatus of ONTOΣ XIV: proofs for XV.1 and XV.3, and the declared Bridge Law (XV.2) with its falsifier discipline. It introduces no new primitive machinery: the category $\mathsf{NestedSub}$ of nested substrate pairs, the Independent-Exhaustion discriminator, the cross-layer cost functional with its X+M decomposition, the topological witness functor $\mathcal{T}$, and the formal definition of Regime W are all imported from the XIV companion. Of the three structural statements, two (XV.1, XV.3) are derived theorems and the third (XV.2) is a declared structural law; Theorem 4 below is the Regime-W-execution corollary of Theorem 3, not a fourth independent statement. **Theorem 1 (Nestability Criterion — formal IE-nestability):** a substrate admits a nested embedding — in the *formal IE-witness* sense (§2 scope note): existence of a formally declared IE-witness lower, not physical hostability — if and only if it carries a subcritical admissible trajectory — a trajectory traversing at least one edge whose cumulative burden stays below capacity; nestability is a property of the budget staying sub-capacity on some genuine motion, orthogonal to state-set cardinality, with the *sealed core* (single edge saturating capacity) as the canonical non-nestable witness of positive size. **Theorem 2 (Spin as Maintenance Geometry — a declared structural law):** in the boundary-layer-admissible class — a *loop-bearing-envelope* class: the envelope must carry a homologically non-trivial cycle, so not every smooth boundary layer qualifies — the maintenance-of-nesting cost $M^{\mathrm{cost}}_{S_i}(\nu, e)$ is *declared proportional*, up to a deployment-declared constant, to the magnitude of a declared boundary functional of the spin one-form's envelope realisation on the nested embedding — a structural bridge law (not a derivation from lower axioms) giving the X+M ledger's maintenance component a measurable geometric carrier. **Theorem 3 (Core-Reduction Annihilates Witness)** and **Theorem 4 (Core-Reduction Executes Regime W under Preservation Conditions):** a transport scheme whose carrier-map factors through a contractible core induces the zero map on first cohomology, so it cannot preserve a non-trivial identity witness; when the post-event carrier is the contractible core itself (or deformation-retracts to it), the witness is moreover annihilated, satisfying condition (4) of the formal Regime W of XIV. The folding map of the XIV companion's Theorem 10, read as a transport scheme, is exactly such a core-reduction. **Proposition 4.3 (Witness Transport Kernel Criterion)** generalises this: a transport annihilates the witness (in the pairing sense) exactly when its induced map sends every witness-bearing recurrence class into the pairing kernel — a contractible core is the canonical instance and Theorem 3(i) is the corollary, but contractibility is sufficient, not necessary: a transport can annihilate relative to the target witness while the transported classes survive, admitting no contractible factor at all, and a degree-zero map of a non-contractible carrier annihilates identically with non-contractible pre- and post-carriers (its winding-zero walk secretly lifting through a contractible factor). The three statements close on the two orthogonal axes of XIV's death map — budget and witness — with Theorem 2 as the bridge between them. A further result (Proposition 3.7, Boundary–Witness Compatibility) identifies a declared subclass on which the net-circulation equals the transported witness period, refining the register of the maintenance geometry without altering the declared status of the Bridge Law. A layer of refinements accompanies the three statements — among them the *nestability margin* (Proposition 2.4), which turns Theorem 1 into a perturbation-stable criterion and bounds the robustness of every nesting over the substrate (Proposition 2.7); the *autonomy ladder* (Definition 2.5), which converts Theorem 1's formal scope note into a claim hierarchy; the *multi-event $c$-consistency falsifier* (Proposition 3.8), sharpened on the compatible subclass *with integral witness data* to *maintenance quantisation with a positive floor* — no witness-bearing compatible nesting is maintenance-free, and costs must sit on the quantum lattice (Proposition 3.11); the *channel inequality* $\Lambda_{\mathrm{loc}} \ge |\sigma_H|$ (Lemma 3.12; counting measure, and any measure datum $w \ge 1$), sharpened to the *minimal-entrainment bound* (Proposition 3.14) — a derived variational floor with exact minimisers, under which, within a named effort reading of the local channel (norm or quadratic), the *form* of the minimal maintenance cost is computed from the locus geometry rather than declared, the Bridge Law itself for general deployments remaining declared; the *two-channel feasibility falsifier* (Proposition 3.15), developing Remark 3.10's joint discipline into an exact two-parameter test; the *witness capacity bound* — a transport chain carries at most as many independent witness classes as the smallest first Betti number it passes through (Proposition 4.6); and the *retention index* with the *composition calculus* (Remarks 4.4–4.5, Proposition 4.7), which grade the transport trichotomy. Two further layers quantify the nesting dynamics and the epistemic boundary: the *drift layer* (Definition 2.9, Proposition 2.10) — under a declared window discipline with sealed per-window bands, Independent-Exhaustion is *forced* within the derived horizon $\lceil 1/a \rceil$ at bounded image cost — the pushforward strictly subcritical whenever $\lceil 1/a \rceil \cdot \varepsilon < 1$ — rather than witnessed by example, the bands reading as discrete dissipation inequalities (a named structural parallel, not an inheritance); and the *source-localisation floor* (Definition 5.3, Proposition 5.4) — under a declared finite observation channel, every lower-side estimator of the upper source errs with probability bounded below by an explicit information-theoretic floor, the mirror direction of the imported HF-NR bound — the two combining into the *imposition certificate* (Remark 5.5): forced structural effect at bounded upper cost, with source localisation bounded away from certainty. ## 1. Preliminaries: imported apparatus

ONTOΣ XV introduces no new primitive machinery. This section fixes the imports by reference, with the cross-references the proofs below depend on. No import is re-derived; each is cited to its source.

### 1.1. Substrate and nesting apparatus (from XIV companion §2–§3)

- **Substrate object** (XIV companion Definition 2.1): a tuple $\mathfrak{S} = (X, \mathsf{D}_S, \mathrm{Adm}_S, \Phi_S, C_S)$ with $X$ a state set, $\mathsf{D}_S$ a dynamical skeleton, $\mathrm{Adm}_S \subseteq \mathrm{Traj}_{\mathsf{D}_S}$ an admissibility predicate satisfying the pre-safety axioms (shift-invariance, concat-decomposition, window-restriction), $\Phi_S \colon \mathrm{Traj}_{\mathsf{D}_S} \to [0, \infty]$ a burden functional monotone non-decreasing under trajectory extension with $\Phi_S((x)) := 0$ on any zero-length trajectory (a single state, no edges), and $C_S \in (0, \infty]$ the capacity. The budget is $\tau_S(\gamma) := C_S - \Phi_S(\gamma)$. The substrate category $\mathsf{Sub}$ has these as objects and budget-respecting admissibility-preserving maps as morphisms (XIV companion Definition 2.1').
- **Nested substrate pair** (XIV companion Definition 2.2): a triple $(\mathfrak{S}, \mathfrak{S}_i, \nu)$ where $\nu \colon \mathfrak{S}_i \to \mathfrak{S}$ is an $\mathsf{AdmDyn}$-morphism (admissibility-preserving) whose underlying state-set map is *injective* — making $\nu$ monic — but which is *deliberately not required to be budget-respecting*. This last point is load-bearing for the present work: the construction of Theorem 1 below builds a nesting morphism that is admissibility-preserving and monic, and it relies on the freedom from the budget-respecting condition that Definition 2.2 grants. The genuinely-nested subcategory $\mathsf{NestedSub}_{\mathrm{nested}}$ is carved by the Independent-Exhaustion criterion below.
- **Independent-Exhaustion criterion** (XIV companion Definition 3.1): the pair satisfies IE iff there exists an admissible trajectory $\gamma_i$ of $\mathfrak{S}_i$ with $\Phi_{S_i}(\gamma_i) \ge C_{S_i}$ at first crossing while the pushforward $\nu_* \gamma_i$ has $\Phi_S(\nu_* \gamma_i) < C_S$ — the lower budget exhausts while the upper stays strictly positive (forward-only direction, XIV companion §3.4). Remark 3.1.1 of XIV records that $C_{S_i} = \infty$ excludes IE vacuously.
- **Sealed deployment declaration** (XIV companion Definition 2.6): a substrate's $(\Phi_S, C_S, Y_S, \mathsf{evid}_S)$ are declared and sealed before any cross-layer event observation. The constant $c$ of Theorem 2 and the boundary-layer admissibility claim of §3 are sealed under the same discipline.

## 2. The Nestability Criterion (XV.1)

The Independent-Exhaustion criterion (XIV companion Definition 3.1) discriminates a *given* candidate pair: it decides whether $(\mathfrak{S}, \mathfrak{S}_i, \nu)$ is genuinely nested or role-bound. The prior question — whether a given upper substrate $\mathfrak{S}$ admits *any* nested lower at all — was left to the deployment-declaration discipline in XIV. This section characterises it as a property of $\mathfrak{S}$ alone, and the characterisation is an iff.

**Definition 2.1 (Subcritical admissible trajectory).** A *subcritical admissible trajectory* of a substrate object $\mathfrak{S} = (X, \mathsf{D}_S, \mathrm{Adm}_S, \Phi_S, C_S)$ is an admissible trajectory $\gamma \in \mathrm{Adm}_S$ that traverses *at least one edge* (i.e. is not a zero-length single-state trajectory) and satisfies $\Phi_S(\gamma) < C_S$.

The two conditions are independent: the edge-count condition excludes the trivial zero-length trajectory (which has $\Phi_S = 0 < C_S$ vacuously by the empty-sum convention and would otherwise make every substrate "subcritical"), and the strict inequality $\Phi_S(\gamma) < C_S$ excludes a trajectory that saturates capacity at the step under consideration. A substrate carries a subcritical trajectory exactly when its burden stays *below* capacity on some genuine (non-empty) motion, leaving strictly positive budget. In the generic case of positive step-burdens this value is strictly between zero and capacity ($0 < \Phi_S(\gamma) < C_S$); a degenerate zero-cost genuine motion ($\Phi_S(\gamma) = 0$) also qualifies, since it too leaves the budget below saturation. The sealed core (Counter-example 2.2) fails because its only genuine motion *saturates* the capacity, not because it merely lacks a strictly-intermediate value.

**Theorem 1 (Nestability Criterion — formal IE-nestability).** A substrate object $\mathfrak{S}$ *admits a nested embedding* — in the formal IE-witness sense of the scope note below — i.e. there exist a substrate object $\mathfrak{S}_i$ and a nesting morphism $\nu$ such that $(\mathfrak{S}, \mathfrak{S}_i, \nu) \in \mathsf{NestedSub}_{\mathrm{nested}}$ — *if and only if* $\mathfrak{S}$ carries a subcritical admissible trajectory (Definition 2.1).

**Proof.**

*($\Leftarrow$) Subcritical trajectory implies nestability.* Let $\gamma \in \mathrm{Adm}_S$ be subcritical: it traverses $k \ge 1$ edges and $\Phi_S(\gamma) < C_S$. Construct an inner substrate $\mathfrak{S}_i$ carried by $\gamma$:

- *State set.* $X_i :=$ the set of states visited by $\gamma$, a finite subset of $X$; let $\iota \colon X_i \hookrightarrow X$ be the subset inclusion (injective).
- *Dynamical skeleton.* $\mathsf{D}_{S_i} :=$ the sub-skeleton of $\mathsf{D}_S$ whose edges are exactly the edges traversed by $\gamma$; then $\gamma$ is a trajectory of $\mathsf{D}_{S_i}$.
- *Admissibility.* $\mathrm{Adm}_{S_i} := \{\pi_{[a,b]}\gamma : [a,b] \text{ a window of } \gamma\}$, the set of all window-restrictions of $\gamma$. This predicate is window-restriction-closed by construction (a window of a window is a window), shift-invariant on its members, and concat-decomposable in the one-sided sense; the pre-safety axioms of [admissibility] hold. In particular $\gamma \in \mathrm{Adm}_{S_i}$.
- *Burden.* Assign per-edge step-burden $1$ to each edge of $\mathsf{D}_{S_i}$; then $\Phi_{S_i}(\delta)$ is the number of steps of $\delta$ — edge-traversals counted with multiplicity, so a trajectory that revisits an edge pays for each pass, not once per distinct edge. This is monotone non-decreasing under trajectory extension, with $\Phi_{S_i}((x)) = 0$ on zero-length trajectories.
- *Capacity.* $C_{S_i} := k = |\gamma|$ (the step count of $\gamma$, traversals counted with multiplicity), which lies in $(0, \infty)$ since $k \ge 1$.
- *Nesting morphism.* $\nu := \iota$ as an $\mathsf{AdmDyn}$-morphism, acting edgewise: $\nu_*\delta$ is the trajectory $\delta$ read inside $\mathfrak{S}$. It is *admissibility-preserving*: every $\delta \in \mathrm{Adm}_{S_i}$ is a window-restriction $\pi_{[a,b]}\gamma$, and $\nu_*\delta = \pi_{[a,b]}\gamma$ is a window-restriction of $\gamma \in \mathrm{Adm}_S$, hence lies in $\mathrm{Adm}_S$ by the window-restriction pre-safety axiom of $\mathfrak{S}$ (XIV companion Definition 2.1). Its underlying state map $\iota$ is injective, so $\nu$ is monic. No budget-respecting condition is required of $\nu$ (XIV companion Definition 2.2), so the construction is well-typed.

We verify the Independent-Exhaustion criterion (XIV companion Definition 3.1) on the witness $\gamma_i := \gamma \in \mathrm{Adm}_{S_i}$. Its cumulative burden $\Phi_{S_i}(\gamma_i) = k = C_{S_i}$ reaches the lower capacity at the final edge (and is strictly below it before), so $\Phi_{S_i}(\gamma_i) \ge C_{S_i}$ at first crossing. The pushforward $\nu_*\gamma_i = \gamma$ satisfies $\Phi_S(\nu_*\gamma_i) = \Phi_S(\gamma) < C_S$ by hypothesis, hence $\tau_S(\nu_*\gamma_i) > 0$. Both conditions of XIV companion Definition 3.1 hold, so $(\mathfrak{S}, \mathfrak{S}_i, \nu) \in \mathsf{NestedSub}_{\mathrm{nested}}$. Thus $\mathfrak{S}$ admits a nested embedding.

*($\Rightarrow$) Nestability implies a subcritical trajectory.* Suppose $(\mathfrak{S}, \mathfrak{S}_i, \nu) \in \mathsf{NestedSub}_{\mathrm{nested}}$ for some inner substrate and nesting morphism. By XIV companion Definition 3.1 there is an admissible $\gamma_i \in \mathrm{Adm}_{S_i}$ with $\Phi_{S_i}(\gamma_i) \ge C_{S_i}$ at first crossing and $\Phi_S(\nu_*\gamma_i) < C_S$. The witness $\gamma_i$ traverses at least one edge: a zero-length trajectory has $\Phi_{S_i} = 0$ (empty-sum convention), and since $C_{S_i} > 0$ (XIV companion Definition 2.1, capacity $C_S \in (0, \infty]$) it could not meet the first-crossing condition $\Phi_{S_i} \ge C_{S_i} > 0$. Because the trajectory action of an $\mathsf{AdmDyn}$-morphism is *pointwise on state sequences* — $\nu_*$ sends $(s_0, s_1, \dots)$ to $(\nu s_0, \nu s_1, \dots)$, preserving the step count by construction (XIV companion Definition 2.2 and its worked usage: $\nu_*(s_0, s_1) = (w_0, w_1)$ in its §7.1) — each transition $s \to s'$ is sent to the transition $\nu(s) \to \nu(s')$, and state-injectivity additionally keeps distinct states distinct (a self-loop maps to a self-loop; no step is ever dropped); hence the pushforward of a trajectory traversing at least one edge again traverses at least one edge, so $\nu_*\gamma_i$ is non-empty. It is admissible in $\mathfrak{S}$ (admissibility-preservation of $\nu$) and satisfies $\Phi_S(\nu_*\gamma_i) < C_S$. Therefore $\gamma := \nu_*\gamma_i$ is a subcritical admissible trajectory of $\mathfrak{S}$. $\blacksquare$

The construction in the ($\Leftarrow$) direction is the minimal one: the inner substrate is the trajectory itself, equipped with the unit per-edge burden and capacity equal to its length, embedded by inclusion. It is not the only nesting a subcritical trajectory licenses; it suffices to witness existence. The construction is also repetition-robust — nothing requires $\gamma$ to be simple. If $\gamma$ revisits states or edges, the collapse happens only in the carrier sets ($X_i$ and the sub-skeleton record each visited state and traversed edge once), while the witness arithmetic lives on the time-indexed trajectory itself: the admissibility predicate is the set of window-restrictions of $\gamma$, and the burden counts steps, so the cumulative burden of $\gamma$ is its length $k$, with first crossing at the final step whatever the repetition pattern. No time-indexed duplication of states is needed: the witness is the trajectory, not the visited set.

**Scope of Theorem 1 (formal IE-nestability).** The criterion characterises the existence of a *formally declared IE-witness* lower substrate over a subcritical trajectory, under the current $\mathsf{NestedSub}$ typing — where $\nu$ need not be budget-respecting (XIV companion Definition 2.2) and the inner capacity is free. It does *not*, by itself, certify physical independence of the lower substrate, epistemic autonomy, non-fabricated lower dynamics, or a witness-bearing identity: the minimal construction above *fabricates* the inner substrate from the trajectory. "$\mathfrak{S}$ admits a nested embedding" is read in this formal IE-witness sense throughout; promoting it to physical hostability requires the deployment-side independence audit of XIV companion §6.7, not Theorem 1 alone.

**Counter-example 2.2 (The sealed core).** Let $\mathfrak{S}_\bullet$ have state set $X = \{a, b\}$, a single edge $(a, b)$ with $\Phi_S(a \to b) = 1$, capacity $C_S = 1$, and $\mathrm{Adm}_S$ the stay-in-$K$ predicate with $K = X$ (every trajectory of the skeleton is admissible). The burden functional takes exactly two values: $0$ on the zero-length trajectories $(a)$ and $(b)$, and $1$ on the single edge-traversing trajectory $(a, b)$. The only trajectory that traverses an edge has $\Phi_S = 1 = C_S$, *not* strictly below $C_S$. Hence $\mathfrak{S}_\bullet$ carries no subcritical admissible trajectory, and by Theorem 1 it admits no nested embedding — every candidate pair built over it lands in $\mathsf{NestedSub}_{\mathrm{role}}$, not $\mathsf{NestedSub}_{\mathrm{nested}}$. Yet $\mathfrak{S}_\bullet$ has positive size: two distinct states, a live transition, a strictly positive capacity. We call such a substrate a *sealed core*: every non-zero motion burns the full capacity in one stroke, leaving no intermediate budget for an inner substrate to occupy.

**Proposition 2.3 (Nestability is orthogonal to size).** Nestability is neither necessary nor sufficient for a substrate to have more than one state.

*Proof.* *Not sufficient:* the sealed core (Counter-example 2.2) has two states and is not nestable. *Not necessary:* let $\mathfrak{S}_\circ$ have a single state $x$ with a self-loop $(x, x)$ of burden $\Phi_S(x \to x) = m$ and capacity $C_S > m > 0$. The one-edge trajectory $(x, x)$ traverses an edge and has $\Phi_S = m < C_S$, so it is subcritical; by Theorem 1, $\mathfrak{S}_\circ$ admits a nested embedding, while having only one state. $\square$

**Proposition 2.4 (Nestability Margin).** Work in the finite-state substrate category $\mathsf{Sub}$ (Theorem 1's scope), with finite capacity $C_S \in (0, \infty)$, and suppose $\mathfrak{S}$ carries at least one non-empty admissible trajectory (otherwise no subcritical trajectory exists, $\mathfrak{S}$ is not nestable by Theorem 1, and the margin below is undefined). The *nestability margin* of $\mathfrak{S}$ is
$$
\mu_{\mathrm{nest}}(\mathfrak{S}) := 1 - \frac{1}{C_S} \min\bigl\{ \Phi_S(\gamma) : \gamma \in \mathrm{Adm}_S,\ |\gamma| \ge 1 \bigr\}.
$$
The minimum is attained: by the window-restriction pre-safety axiom (XIV companion Definition 2.1) the first edge of any admissible trajectory is itself admissible and, by monotonicity of $\Phi_S$, no costlier, so the minimum ranges over the finitely many admissible single-edge trajectories of the finite skeleton. Then:

(a) $\mathfrak{S}$ admits a nested embedding (Theorem 1) *if and only if* $\mu_{\mathrm{nest}}(\mathfrak{S}) > 0$;

(b) *(Robustness.)* if $\mu_{\mathrm{nest}}(\mathfrak{S}) \ge \delta > 0$, then every substrate $\mathfrak{S}'$ obtained from $\mathfrak{S}$ by replacing $\Phi_S$ with a burden functional $\Phi'_S$ satisfying the same axioms and with $\bigl| \Phi'_S(\gamma) - \Phi_S(\gamma) \bigr| < \delta C_S$ on every admissible single-edge trajectory $\gamma$ — states, skeleton, admissibility, and capacity unchanged — remains nestable. (In the edge-weighted reading: each per-edge burden shifted by less than $\delta C_S$, burdens held $\ge 0$.)

*Proof.* By Theorem 1, $\mathfrak{S}$ is nestable iff it carries a subcritical trajectory — a non-empty admissible $\gamma$ with $\Phi_S(\gamma) < C_S$ — which, by the single-edge reduction above, holds iff $\min\{\Phi_S(\gamma) : \gamma \in \mathrm{Adm}_S,\ |\gamma| \ge 1\} < C_S$, i.e. iff $\mu_{\mathrm{nest}} > 0$; this is (a). For (b): a cheapest admissible single edge $\gamma_\ast$ has $\Phi_S(\gamma_\ast) \le (1 - \delta) C_S = C_S - \delta C_S$, so $\Phi'_S(\gamma_\ast) < C_S$; $\gamma_\ast$ remains admissible (admissibility and the skeleton are unchanged — only the burden functional moved), so $\mathfrak{S}'$ carries a subcritical trajectory and is nestable by (a). $\square$

The margin is a *finite-capacity* quantity: at $C_S = \infty$ it is $1$ for any finite-burden motion, matching the degeneracy of the criterion (§6), so it presumes $C_S < \infty$. It records not merely *whether* $\mathfrak{S}$ is nestable but *how much room* the budget leaves: the sealed core (Counter-example 2.2) has $\mu_{\mathrm{nest}} = 0$ (its only motion saturates), a zero-cost genuine motion gives $\mu_{\mathrm{nest}} = 1$ (distinct from the excluded zero-*length* trajectory), and the robustness clause turns the binary criterion into a perturbation-stable one. The constant in (b) is *sharp*: on the subcritical self-loop with burden $m$ and capacity $C_S$, a per-edge shift of exactly $\delta C_S = C_S - m$ raises the only genuine motion to saturation and nestability is lost — the strict inequality cannot be weakened.

**Definition 2.5 (Autonomous nested embedding).** Theorem 1's scope note records that its criterion is *formal IE-nestability*: the minimal construction fabricates the inner substrate from the witnessing trajectory. The next rung of the ladder is structural, not a caveat. A nested embedding $(\mathfrak{S}_i, \nu)$ of $\mathfrak{S}$ is *autonomous* iff, in addition to $(\mathfrak{S}, \mathfrak{S}_i, \nu) \in \mathsf{NestedSub}_{\mathrm{nested}}$:

- **(A-i)** the inner declaration $(X_i, \mathsf{D}_{S_i}, \mathrm{Adm}_{S_i}, \Phi_{S_i}, C_{S_i})$, *together with its spin/witness data* $(\omega_{S_i}, [\eta_{S_i}])$, is sealed *before* the witnessing event is scored (the discipline of XIV companion Definition 2.6, applied to the lower substrate — the witness datum is inside the seal, so it cannot be fabricated after the event);
- **(A-ii)** neither $\Phi_{S_i}$ nor $C_{S_i}$ is extracted from the witnessing trajectory itself (in particular, no capacity set to the witness's own length, as the minimal construction does);
- **(A-iii)** the declared data support a *witness-bearing recurrence*: a class $[\gamma_{\mathrm{rec}}]$ on the identity-supporting carrier with $\langle [\eta_{S_i}], [\gamma_{\mathrm{rec}}] \rangle \neq 0$ (the apparatus of §1.3; this forces $b_1 \ge 1$ on the carrier).

This yields a three-rung ladder: *formal IE-nestability* (Theorem 1) $\supsetneq$ *autonomous nestability* $\supseteq$ *witness-bearing hostability* (the deployment-audited rung, XIV companion §6.7 — autonomy plus the physical-independence audit). Strictness of the first inclusion is Proposition 2.6; the second is a *deployment-level* distinction — the audit is a prose/deployment criterion, not a statement of this apparatus, so no strictness claim is made for it here.

**Proposition 2.6 (The ladder is strict at its first rung).** Every autonomous embedding is formal; the converse fails, and it fails already on Theorem 1's own construction. *Proof.* Autonomy adds conditions, so autonomous $\Rightarrow$ formal. For strictness, the load-bearing leg is (A-iii), a property of the mathematical object itself: when $\gamma$ is a simple (non-self-crossing) trajectory, the fabricated inner substrate has no recurrent orbit at all — its recurrence graph is edgeless, $b_1 = 0$ — so no witness-bearing recurrence class exists and (A-iii) fails, whatever the deployment record says. Conditions (A-i)–(A-ii) fail additionally at the deployment-register level: the minimal construction assembles the inner substrate from the witnessing trajectory after the fact, with $C_{S_i} := |\gamma|$ (the same abstract object *could* have been declared pre-event, which is why the object-level leg (A-iii) carries the proof). A declared cyclic lower ($b_1 \ge 1$) sealed before the event, by contrast, can satisfy all three. Hence the inclusion is strict. $\square$

The ladder converts the scope note of Theorem 1 from a disclaimer into a measurable claim-hierarchy: XV.1 characterises the *bottom* rung exactly (the iff); the upper rungs are where deployment-level content lives, and a deployment claiming more than formal nestability must state which rung it certifies.

**Proposition 2.7 (Margin dominance).** Let $\mathfrak{S}$ have finite capacity and let $(\mathfrak{S}, \mathfrak{S}_i, \nu) \in \mathsf{NestedSub}_{\mathrm{nested}}$ be *any* nested pair over it, with IE witness $\gamma_i$ (XIV companion Definition 3.1). Then
$$
\mu_{\mathrm{nest}}(\mathfrak{S}) \cdot C_S \;\ge\; \tau_S(\nu_* \gamma_i) \;=\; C_S - \Phi_S(\nu_* \gamma_i) \;>\; 0 ,
$$
and consequently $\mu_{\mathrm{nest}}(\mathfrak{S}) \cdot C_S \ge m_{\mathrm{IE}}$, the IE-robustness margin of the pair (the FD-1 margin of the XIV robustness companion, whose value is a minimum whose first argument is $\tau_S(\nu_* \gamma_i)$ at the maximising witness). The nestability margin is thus a *substrate-intrinsic ceiling* on the upper-budget slack — hence on the pair-level robustness — of everything that can nest in $\mathfrak{S}$.

*Proof.* $\nu_* \gamma_i$ is a non-empty admissible trajectory of $\mathfrak{S}$ (Theorem 1's $(\Rightarrow)$ argument) with $\Phi_S(\nu_* \gamma_i) < C_S$; the minimum in Proposition 2.4 runs over all non-empty admissible trajectories, so $\min \Phi_S \le \Phi_S(\nu_* \gamma_i)$, i.e. $\mu_{\mathrm{nest}} C_S = C_S - \min \Phi_S \ge \tau_S(\nu_* \gamma_i)$. For the second claim, note first that the FD-1 maximum may be taken over *non-empty* lower trajectories: a zero-length $\gamma$ scores $-\tau_{S_i}(\gamma) = -C_{S_i} < 0$ in the minimum, so it never realises a non-negative $m_{\mathrm{IE}}$. On non-empty $\gamma$ the images are non-empty admissible motions, so $m_{\mathrm{IE}} \le \max_{\gamma \neq \emptyset} \tau_S(\nu_* \gamma) \le \mu_{\mathrm{nest}} C_S$. The *first* bound is tight: a lower substrate whose witness's image is a cheapest admissible motion of $\mathfrak{S}$ has upper-budget slack exactly $\mu_{\mathrm{nest}} C_S$ (attaining the $m_{\mathrm{IE}}$ bound as well requires, in addition, the witness's own overshoot to reach that value). $\square$

**[ALIGNMENT]** *Cross-corpus reading.* Proposition 2.7 is the quantitative bridge between XV.1's eligibility axis and the robustness apparatus of the XIV bundle: the margin introduced here (a property of $\mathfrak{S}$ alone) dominates the pair-level IE margin defined there, so a substrate with a thin margin cannot host a robust nesting no matter how the pair is engineered.

**Remark 2.8 (Linear-time decidability).** In finite-state $\mathsf{Sub}$, nestability and $\mu_{\mathrm{nest}}$ are computed by a single scan of the admissible single-edge trajectories — $O(|E|)$ — since Proposition 2.4's minimum is attained there. The eligibility question of XV.1 is not merely decidable; it is decidable at the cost of reading the substrate once.

**Definition 2.9 (Declared drift class).** Let $(\mathfrak{S}, \mathfrak{S}_i, \nu)$ be a nested-pair *candidate* — a triple per XIV companion Definition 2.2, with $\nu$ admissibility-preserving and monic, *not* presupposed to lie in the genuinely-nested subcategory — with finite capacities $C_S, C_{S_i} \in (0, \infty)$. A *drift declaration* for the pair is a window rule — a scheme assigning to an admissible trajectory $\gamma_i$ of $\mathfrak{S}_i$ a decomposition into consecutive windows $w_1, \dots, w_m$ (concat-decomposition of the pre-safety axioms; every prefix $\gamma_i|_{w_1 \dots w_k}$ is admissible by window-restriction) — together with two constants $a > 0$ and $\varepsilon \ge 0$, all declared and sealed per XIV companion Definition 2.6 and fixed *for the class* under the one-rule-for-the-class discipline of Definition 3.2, never re-chosen trajectory by trajectory. A trajectory $\gamma_i$ *lies in the $(a, \varepsilon)$-drift class* iff for every window index $k$ the prefix differences of the two burdens satisfy
$$\Phi_{S_i}\bigl(\gamma_i|_{w_1 \dots w_k}\bigr) - \Phi_{S_i}\bigl(\gamma_i|_{w_1 \dots w_{k-1}}\bigr) \;\ge\; a \cdot C_{S_i}, \qquad \Phi_S\bigl(\nu_*(\gamma_i|_{w_1 \dots w_k})\bigr) - \Phi_S\bigl(\nu_*(\gamma_i|_{w_1 \dots w_{k-1}})\bigr) \;\le\; \varepsilon \cdot C_S .$$
The increments are *prefix differences* of the monotone burden functionals — well-defined and non-negative with no additivity assumed of $\Phi$ — and the pushforward respects prefixes because the trajectory action of $\nu_*$ is pointwise on state sequences (as in Theorem 1's proof). Membership is a *measured* condition against a *declared* band: a trajectory whose measured windows leave the bands falsifies its own class-membership declaration, in the discipline of Proposition 3.8 — not any statement below.

**Proposition 2.10 (Forced Independent-Exhaustion).** Let $\gamma_i$ lie in the $(a, \varepsilon)$-drift class of a declared drift declaration, extending over at least $N := \lceil 1/a \rceil$ windows. Then:

(i) *(forced crossing.)* The lower burden reaches first crossing, $\Phi_{S_i} \ge C_{S_i}$, no later than the boundary of window $N$;

(ii) *(bounded image cost.)* If the crossing arrives within window $k \le N$, the image burden accumulated to that point satisfies $\Phi_S\bigl(\nu_*(\gamma_i|_{w_1 \dots w_k})\bigr) \le k \cdot \varepsilon \cdot C_S \le N \cdot \varepsilon \cdot C_S$;

(iii) *(forced Independent-Exhaustion.)* If moreover $N \cdot \varepsilon < 1$, the pushforward at the crossing is strictly subcritical, and the first-crossing prefix is an Independent-Exhaustion witness (XIV companion Definition 3.1): the candidate pair lies in $\mathsf{NestedSub}_{\mathrm{nested}}$ — membership is *derived* from the declared class, not presupposed by the typing and not exhibited by a one-off example. For $N \cdot \varepsilon \ge 1$ no subcriticality claim is made — the image can exhaust within the horizon, and the clause is withheld.

*Proof.* The prefix differences of $\Phi_{S_i}$ telescope: the burden of the prefix through window $k$ equals the sum of the first $k$ increments ($\Phi_{S_i}$ of the empty prefix is $0$), so after $N$ windows the cumulative burden is $\ge N \cdot a \cdot C_{S_i} \ge C_{S_i}$, giving (i); the first-crossing prefix is admissible by window-restriction. The image increments telescope identically, giving (ii). For (iii): $N \varepsilon < 1$ gives $\Phi_S(\nu_*\,\text{prefix}) \le N \varepsilon C_S < C_S$, so the pushforward leaves the upper budget strictly positive while the lower has exhausted at first crossing — both conditions of XIV companion Definition 3.1. $\blacksquare$

**[ALIGNMENT]** *The dissipativity reading.* The drift bands are discrete *dissipation inequalities* in the sense of dissipativity theory (supply rates and storage functions — Willems): the normalized burden $\Phi/C$ plays the storage-like reading, and the window bands are supply-rate bounds — the lower substrate is supplied burden at a guaranteed rate while the image's supply stays capped. The maintenance ledger's floors (Propositions 3.11–3.14) and the baseline discipline of §3.2's empirical mode already read as minimal-supply statements in the same vocabulary. This is a structural-pattern parallel, not a logical inheritance: no storage function is constructed here, and a full storage-function typing of the maintenance ledger is a named open direction (Open Problem 7.8).

**Remark 2.11 (Death-mode increment profiles).** The window profile of the *upper* budget separates the death taxonomy of XIV §3-bis at the increment level: *Drain* is the accumulation profile — many sub-capacity windows whose telescoped sum approaches the capacity; *Snap-B* is the one-window profile — a single increment consuming the full remaining budget; and *Regime W* is the flat profile — window increments near zero on the budget while the witness dies on the carrier (Theorem 4): collapse without budget expenditure, the profile-level form of the fact that collapse need not coincide with budget-zero. The typing is bookkeeping over the existing apparatus, not a new death mode.

**[ALIGNMENT]** *Architectural co-witness.* Theorem 1 sits on the budget axis of the XIV death map (XIV companion §10.7, §12). A substrate whose burden admits no value strictly between $0$ and $C_S$ has no *Drain* mode either, in the measure-theoretic sense of XIV companion §7.9: there is no admissible motion that contracts the interior along an expenditure costing strictly less than the remaining capacity. The sealed core is the maximally degenerate case in which every event is, at the substrate's own scale, an instantaneous full-capacity expenditure. Subcritical admissibility — the existence of a strictly intermediate expenditure — is what *Drain* uses and what nesting *needs*; the two are siblings on the same axis. Nestability is the budgetary echo, at the lower substrate's scale, of the upper substrate's having room to be drained gradually rather than only snapped.

## 3. Spin-Channel Bridge (XV.2)

The XIV companion carries two objects side by side without a corridor between them: the imposed-spin field of the XIV essay §3 — the non-gradient component the upper substrate writes into the lower's dynamics — and the maintenance-of-nesting cost $M^{\mathrm{cost}}_{S_i}(\nu, e) \ge 0$ of the XIV companion §9, declared as a scalar kernel. The notation collision on the symbol $M$ was closed at v6.1.1 with the $M^{\mathrm{carrier}}$ / $M^{\mathrm{cost}}$ disambiguation, but the *content* connection — that $M^{\mathrm{cost}}$ has a geometric realisation in the spin field's circulation — was left as the explicit ONTOΣ XV programme noted at XIV companion §11.1. This section builds the corridor.

### 3.1. The maintenance-circulation

**Definition 3.1 (Maintenance-circulation).** Let $(\mathfrak{S}, \mathfrak{S}_i, \nu) \in \mathsf{NestedSub}_{\mathrm{nested}}$ be boundary-layer-admissible (Definition 3.2), supplying a declared oriented $1$-cycle $\Gamma_{\nu, e}$ in the boundary-layer envelope of $\nu(X_i) \subseteq X$, a declared *envelope realisation* $\widetilde{\omega}^{\nu, e}_{S_i}$ of the lower spin one-form $\omega_{S_i} = S_i^\flat$ on a neighbourhood $U_{\nu, e}$ of $\Gamma_{\nu, e}$ in $X$ (Definition 3.2), and a declared boundary functional $\mathcal{N}$. The *maintenance-circulation* of the pair at event $e$ is the value of that functional on the realised one-form over $\Gamma_{\nu, e}$:
$$
\sigma^{(M)}_{S_i}(\nu, e) := \mathcal{N}_{\Gamma_{\nu, e}}\bigl(\widetilde{\omega}^{\nu, e}_{S_i}\bigr).
$$
The realisation is necessary because $\omega_{S_i}$ is natively a one-form of the *lower* carrier, whereas $\Gamma_{\nu, e}$ lives in the *upper* state space $X$: the integration is of the declared upper-space realisation $\widetilde{\omega}^{\nu, e}_{S_i}$, not of $\omega_{S_i}$ directly (which is not typed against an upper cycle). $\sigma^{(M)}$ is *event-indexed* — a scalar attached to the holding of the nesting *through* event $e$, matching the event-indexed signature of the $M^{\mathrm{cost}}$ kernel of XIV companion Definition 9.1 — and is read on the imposed spin component over that holding.

The *canonical* (simplest) functional is the **net circulation** $\mathcal{N} = \oint_{\Gamma_{\nu, e}} \widetilde{\omega}^{\nu, e}_{S_i}$. Because $\widetilde{\omega}^{\nu, e}_{S_i}$ is declared closed on $U_{\nu, e}$ *under this (net-circulation) choice* (Definition 3.2), the net circulation is a *period* $\langle [\widetilde{\omega}^{\nu, e}_{S_i}], [\Gamma_{\nu, e}] \rangle$ — a homology pairing, not a path-dependent integral (Lemma 3.3). In the finite-state setting it is the graph-Hodge circulation along the boundary edge-cycle, the same combinatorial instrument computing the carrier-level witness $[\eta_{S_i}]$ in XIV companion Definition 11.1; in the continuous setting a line integral over $\Gamma_{\nu, e}$.

Net circulation can **cancel** — counter-rotating cells on the envelope can give $\oint \widetilde{\omega}^{\nu, e}_{S_i} = 0$ while the field is non-zero on $\Gamma_{\nu, e}$ — and so undercounts local maintenance. A deployment may therefore instead declare a **norm functional** $\mathcal{N} = \int_{\Gamma_{\nu, e}} |\widetilde{\omega}^{\nu, e}_{S_i}|$, which reads the pointwise magnitude of the realised form along $\Gamma_{\nu, e}$ (no closedness of $\widetilde{\omega}^{\nu, e}_{S_i}$ required) and vanishes iff $\widetilde{\omega}^{\nu, e}_{S_i} = 0$ on $\Gamma_{\nu, e}$; or a **vorticity-flux functional** $\mathcal{N} = \int_{\Sigma} |d\widetilde{\omega}^{\nu, e}_{S_i}|$ over a declared $2$-chain $\Sigma$ with $\partial \Sigma = \Gamma_{\nu, e}$, which requires $\widetilde{\omega}^{\nu, e}_{S_i}$ to be defined and *non-closed* on $\Sigma$ (a closed realisation would give $d\widetilde{\omega}^{\nu, e}_{S_i} \equiv 0$ and a flux identically zero) and vanishes iff $d\widetilde{\omega}^{\nu, e}_{S_i} = 0$ on $\Sigma$. Unlike net circulation, neither cancels spuriously. Closedness of $\widetilde{\omega}^{\nu, e}_{S_i}$ is thus a *per-functional* requirement — mandatory on $U_{\nu, e}$ for the net-circulation period (Lemma 3.3), unused by the norm, and explicitly *excluded on $\Sigma$* by the vorticity-flux. The functional $\mathcal{N}$ is part of the boundary-layer-admissible declaration (Definition 3.2); the Bridge Law (Theorem 2) holds for the declared $\mathcal{N}$. The *name* maintenance-circulation is inherited from the canonical net-circulation choice; under the norm and vorticity-flux functionals $\sigma^{(M)}$ is a *boundary maintenance reading* rather than a circulation in the strict sense — where the distinction is load-bearing, the two-channel notation of Remark 3.10 ($\sigma_H$ for the period, $\Lambda_{\mathrm{loc}}$ for the magnitude reading) keeps the channels apart.

The maintenance-circulation re-aims the witness instrument: the carrier-level witness $[\eta_{S_i}]$ pairs $\omega_{S_i}$ with the *recurrence* cycles of $M^{\mathrm{carrier}}_{S_i}$ on the lower carrier; the maintenance-circulation reads the *declared realisation* of the same field over the *boundary* cycle $\Gamma_{\nu, e}$ in the upper substrate. One field, two loci, two pairings — not one shared cohomology class (Remark 3.3a).

**Definition 3.2 (Boundary-layer-admissible class).** A nested pair $(\mathfrak{S}, \mathfrak{S}_i, \nu) \in \mathsf{NestedSub}_{\mathrm{nested}}$ is *boundary-layer-admissible* iff the upper substrate's coupling topology furnishes a *boundary-layer envelope* around the embedding, and the deployment declares and seals (per XIV companion Definition 2.6):
- an **oriented $1$-cycle** $\Gamma_{\nu, e} \in Z_1$ in that envelope (a boundary edge-cycle in the finite-state graph reading; an oriented closed curve in the continuous reading), *non-contractible in the envelope and non-trivial in its real homology*, $[\Gamma_{\nu, e}] \neq 0 \in H_1(U_{\nu, e}; \mathbb{R})$ — required so the **net-circulation** period is not *forced to vanish for homological reasons* (a null-homologous $\Gamma$ — every contractible one and, in the continuous reading, also a non-contractible commutator-type loop — pairs to zero with every closed form; homological non-triviality is what makes a non-zero period *possible*, and in the finite-graph reading a non-zero element of $Z_1$ has it automatically — whether the measured period is in fact non-zero remains a measured condition, not a class guarantee). The norm and vorticity-flux functionals read a field magnitude and can be non-zero even on a contractible cycle; the class uniformly declares a non-contractible $\Gamma$ so the canonical net-circulation reading is never homologically degenerate;
- an **envelope realisation** $\widetilde{\omega}^{\nu, e}_{S_i} \in \Omega^1(U_{\nu, e})$ of the lower spin one-form on a neighbourhood $U_{\nu, e}$ of $\Gamma_{\nu, e}$ in $X$ — a declared extension / pushforward operator carrying $\omega_{S_i} = S_i^\flat$ from the lower carrier to an integrable upper-space one-form near $\Gamma_{\nu, e}$, declared as **one sealed realisation rule for the whole declared class**: the operator is fixed before any event is scored and applied per event through the embedding geometry $(\nu, e)$, never re-declared event by event (a per-event re-declaration would reduce the class-level test of Proposition 3.8 to a sequence of one-event fits) — with a *per-functional* regularity requirement (Definition 3.1): **closed** on $U_{\nu, e}$ under the net-circulation choice (so that its period depends only on $[\Gamma_{\nu, e}]$, Lemma 3.3); no closedness under the norm choice; and, under the vorticity-flux choice, defined on the declared $2$-chain $\Sigma$ and *non-closed* there (else the flux vanishes identically);
- a **boundary functional** $\mathcal{N}$ (Definition 3.1) — net circulation, or a norm / vorticity-flux functional;
- for the **norm and vorticity-flux** choices, a sealed **measure datum** used by the magnitude and the integral: the counting measure over the edges of $\Gamma_{\nu, e}$ (resp. the faces of $\Sigma$) in the finite-state reading — the default — or a declared metric / measure on $\Gamma_{\nu, e}$ (resp. $\Sigma$) in the continuous reading (the net-circulation period needs no such datum: it is a topological pairing);
- the **constant** $c$ of the Bridge Law (Theorem 2) — under the fully-sealed protocol, declared before any event observation; under the disclosed calibration-sealed protocol of Proposition 3.8's discipline, frozen before any *scored* event, with the calibration set excluded from scoring (the declaration completes — and this seal applies — at the freeze).

The same one-rule-for-the-class discipline governs the *selection* of the per-event loci: the cycle $\Gamma_{\nu, e}$ and the measure datum vary per event as data, but their selection rules are fixed for the class, not re-chosen event by event — the anti-refit rationale of the realisation clause applies to them with equal force. The realisation datum is what makes $\sigma^{(M)}$ *typed*: $\omega_{S_i}$ is a form of the lower carrier and is not, by itself, integrable against an upper-space cycle; the declared $\widetilde{\omega}^{\nu, e}_{S_i}$ supplies the upper-space one-form that is. The topological boundary of $\nu(X_i)$ need not itself be a $1$-cycle — in dimension $> 2$ it is a hypersurface, against which a $1$-form is not integrable — so the integration locus is the *declared* non-contractible oriented $1$-cycle $\Gamma_{\nu, e}$, not the boundary as such. A pair whose envelope admits no integrable homologically non-trivial $1$-cycle, or no realisation $\widetilde{\omega}$ meeting the declared functional's regularity requirement (e.g. a Cantor-set boundary, or a contractible-only envelope), is *nested* but outside the boundary-layer-admissible class (Open Problem 7.3). Class membership is a *structural* condition — the cycle, the realisation, and the typed functional exist and are sealed; a *measured* value $\sigma^{(M)} = 0$ on an in-class pair is not a membership failure but data for the falsifier (Proposition 3.8: under the norm functional it is the zero-spin control).

*Design note (one class, not two).* A two-class architecture — a *period-admissible* class for the homological reading and a weaker *local-boundary-readable* class for the norm / flux readings — would be a permissible refactoring; the class is deliberately kept single, with the non-contractible $\Gamma$ required uniformly, so that every in-class pair supports the canonical homological reading alongside whatever functional it declares, and the local-only regime is carried by the two-channel refinement (Remark 3.10, Proposition 3.15) rather than by a weaker membership condition. Simply-connected envelopes are thereby out of class *by design*: they enter the bundle's protocol surface as the non-admissible negative control (the protocol companion, §1), not as Bridge-Law instances with a degenerate reading.

**Lemma 3.3 (Invariance of the net-circulation functional).** Under the *net-circulation* choice of $\mathcal{N}$, $\sigma^{(M)}_{S_i}(\nu, e)$ is well-defined and depends only on the homology class $[\Gamma_{\nu, e}]$ of the declared cycle and the de Rham class $[\widetilde{\omega}^{\nu, e}_{S_i}]$ of the declared envelope realisation on $U_{\nu, e}$ — not on the choice of representative cycle or of closed representative of the form.

*Proof.* By Definition 3.2, under the net-circulation choice $\widetilde{\omega}^{\nu, e}_{S_i}$ is declared closed on $U_{\nu, e}$. By Stokes' theorem (de Rham case) / the graph coboundary identity (finite-state case), the integral of a closed form over a cycle is unchanged under (i) replacing the cycle by a homologous one in $U_{\nu, e}$ — two homologous cycles bound a region on which $d\widetilde{\omega}^{\nu, e}_{S_i} = 0$ — and (ii) replacing $\widetilde{\omega}^{\nu, e}_{S_i}$ by $\widetilde{\omega}^{\nu, e}_{S_i} + df$, since $\oint df = 0$ over a cycle. Hence the net circulation is the period $\langle [\widetilde{\omega}^{\nu, e}_{S_i}], [\Gamma_{\nu, e}] \rangle$, a function of those two classes alone. $\square$

The norm and vorticity-flux functionals are, by design, *not* class-invariant: they read the magnitude of the realised field on the declared locus — $\widetilde{\omega}^{\nu, e}_{S_i}$ pointwise on $\Gamma_{\nu, e}$, resp. $d\widetilde{\omega}^{\nu, e}_{S_i}$ on $\Sigma$ — which is precisely why they do not spuriously cancel. The class-invariance of Lemma 3.3 is a property of the net-circulation reading alone.

**Remark 3.3a (Distinct locus from the recurrence witness).** The class $[\widetilde{\omega}^{\nu, e}_{S_i}]$ of Lemma 3.3 lives on $U_{\nu, e}$ in the *upper* state space $X$ (it is the class of the *declared realisation*, not of $\omega_{S_i}$ itself); the recurrence witness $[\eta_{S_i}]$ of XIV companion Definition 11.1 lives on the *lower* carrier $M^{\mathrm{carrier}}_{S_i}$. These are cohomology classes on different loci. The maintenance-circulation $\sigma^{(M)}$ and the witness pairing are therefore two readings of the *same spin field* — $\omega_{S_i}$ on the lower carrier (witness), its declared realisation $\widetilde{\omega}^{\nu, e}_{S_i}$ on the upper envelope (maintenance) — over *two different cycles in two different loci*, not one pairing of a single shared cohomology class. Identifying $[\widetilde{\omega}^{\nu, e}_{S_i}]$ with $[\eta_{S_i}]$ would require an additional hypothesis linking the lower carrier to the upper envelope, which the *general* boundary-layer-admissible class does not assert. A declared subclass that does supply such a hypothesis — a sealed map $\rho$ with $\rho_*[\Gamma_{\nu, e}] = [\gamma_i]$ and $[\widetilde{\omega}^{\nu, e}_{S_i}] = \rho^*[\eta_{S_i}]$ — is the *boundary-witness-compatible subclass* of Proposition 3.7 (§3.5); on it the two loci are *related through* $\rho^*$ (the upper period equals the transported lower witness period) but are still *not identified* as one shared class. Loci-identification for the general class remains open (Open Problem 7.7). The unification of §3.3 is "one field, two loci, two pairings", not "one class".

### 3.2. The bridge law

**Theorem 2 (Spin as Maintenance Geometry — the Bridge Law, a declared structural law).** *(Labelled "Theorem 2" by the corpus's running numbering, not a derivation — see the register note after the proof.)* In the boundary-layer-admissible class, the maintenance-of-nesting cost and the magnitude of the maintenance-circulation are proportional:
$$
M^{\mathrm{cost}}_{S_i}(\nu, e) = c \cdot |\sigma^{(M)}_{S_i}(\nu, e)|,
$$
where $c > 0$ is the *maintenance-per-circulation-unit constant*, declared and sealed per XIV companion Definition 2.6. The relation is a *declared structural law* of the class — in the register of the X+M law of XIV companion Theorem 5 / Definition 9.1, not a derivation from lower axioms — and what it delivers is the identification of a measurable geometric carrier for the previously-scalar maintenance kernel. The specific *linear* form (cost $\propto |\sigma^{(M)}|^1$) and the orientation-discarding magnitude $|\cdot|$ are the simplest one-parameter realisation of the maintenance-as-boundary-entrainment reading; they are part of the declaration, not forced by the geometry (a richer monotone law, one retaining orientation, or an affine form with a separately sealed spin-independent baseline intercept, is a permissible alternative declaration — calibration of the form and constant is Open Problem 7.2). Two points follow within the class:

(a) *Measurable signature.* $M^{\mathrm{cost}}$ acquires a measurable signature — the value $\sigma^{(M)} = \mathcal{N}_{\Gamma_{\nu, e}}(\widetilde{\omega}^{\nu, e}_{S_i})$ of the declared boundary functional, computed by the same graph-Hodge / de Rham instrument the corpus already runs for the witness (Definition 3.1) — and is therefore a measurable quantity (the period $\langle [\widetilde{\omega}^{\nu, e}_{S_i}], [\Gamma_{\nu, e}] \rangle$ for the net-circulation choice, Lemma 3.3, or a field-magnitude functional on the declared locus for the norm / flux choices), not a free scalar.

(b) *Admissibility constraint (not automatic).* The sealed declaration $(c, \mathcal{N}, \Gamma_{\nu, e})$ must satisfy the admissible-overhead bound of XIV companion Theorem 6 in separated mode ($\alpha_i = 1$): $c \cdot |\sigma^{(M)}_{S_i}(\nu, e)| \le C_{S_i} - \Phi^{(X)}_{S_i}(\gamma_i)$. This is a constraint the declaration must *meet* to be admissible — it can fail, since $c$ and the measured circulation are independent inputs (cf. XIV companion §9.4, where the unscaled $C_{\mathrm{s}} = 3$ toy gives a default overhead exceeding its bound and must operate in X-only mode); a declaration violating it is inadmissible, not a counterexample to the bridge. Within the bound, the bridge places the bounded maintenance overhead geometrically and cannot exceed the X+M ceiling.

**Proof (of the derived clauses (a)–(b); the law itself is declared, not derived).** Within the boundary-layer-admissible class, the defining structure (Definition 3.2) is that the inner substrate is held entrained in the upper substrate's coupling topology *through the boundary-layer envelope*: what keeps $\nu(X_i)$ nested as the upper substrate carries it through event $e$ is the circulation the envelope carries, not a gradient field it could be held against statically. The maintenance-of-nesting cost $M^{\mathrm{cost}}_{S_i}(\nu, e)$ of XIV companion Definition 9.1 is, by its own definition, the structural work paid to hold the nesting through $e$. The declaration of the class assigns that work to the boundary circulation at the declared rate $c$ per circulation unit, in the simplest linear-magnitude form $M^{\mathrm{cost}} = c \cdot |\sigma^{(M)}|$ — this is the declared structural law, not a derivation (the linear form and the magnitude collapse are declaration choices, as noted above). The right-hand side is well-defined and measurable — for the net-circulation choice depending only on the boundary-neighbourhood class and the boundary cycle (Lemma 3.3), and for the norm / flux choices a field-magnitude reading on the declared locus (Definition 3.1) — giving the measurability of (a). The sealed declaration is subject to the admissible-overhead bound of XIV companion Theorem 6 whenever $\alpha_i = 1$; whether the declared $(c, \mathcal{N}, \Gamma_{\nu, e})$ meets it is the admissibility constraint of (b). $\blacksquare$

On the register: Theorem 2 is a *declared structural law* of the boundary-layer-admissible class, in the register of the X+M law of XIV companion Theorem 5 / Definition 9.1 (itself a declared scoring law, not a derivation from lower axioms). What it delivers is the identification of a measurable geometric carrier for $M^{\mathrm{cost}}$ — the boundary circulation $\sigma^{(M)}$ (a period by Lemma 3.3 for the net-circulation choice, a field-magnitude functional otherwise, Definition 3.1) — together with the admissibility constraint of clause (b); the declared boundary functional $\mathcal{N}$, the linear form, and the constant $c$ are declared, not derived (Open Problem 7.2), and $\sigma^{(M)}$ is read in the upper-state-space neighbourhood, distinct in locus from the recurrence witness (Remark 3.3a). Outside the class — non-smooth, fractal, or absent boundary — the relation is not theorem-form; it retains the structural-statement-under-operational-test register, with the same falsifier applied to a finite-resolution approximation of the boundary (ONTOΣ XV essay §6).

**Two modes (what is declared vs. what is measured).** The Bridge Law has two readings, which the falsifier (essay §6) keeps apart. In *definitional mode* the deployment takes $M^{\mathrm{cost}} := c \cdot |\sigma^{(M)}|$ as the *declaration* of its maintenance kernel through the boundary geometry — a new way to declare $M$, carrying no independent falsifiable content (the Toy-M of §3.4 is this mode: its $c = 1$, $|\sigma^{(M)}| = 1$, $M^{\mathrm{cost}} = 1$ is bookkeeping, not verification). In *empirical mode* the deployment measures $M^{\mathrm{cost}}$ independently (instrumented maintenance work), scored *net of a declared spin-independent baseline* — a sealed reference protocol, canonically the same holding protocol run with the declared drive absent, whose reading is subtracted before scoring, so that background regime-holding overhead is not booked to the spin channel (the bundle's synthetic demonstrator implements exactly this subtraction: its dynamics-side reading is baseline-subtracted against a zero-field run of the same protocol) — seals $c$ beforehand, and measures $\sigma^{(M)}$ separately; only then is the equality falsifiable. Even then no single-event fit counts as evidence for the law: a lone positive-circulation event is always fitted exactly by an unsealed $c$, so support begins where the sealed multi-event discipline begins — one event can *violate* an already-sealed $c$ (Proposition 3.8, clause (a)), but no single event can *support* the proportionality. The corpus claims the law at the declared-structural-law register; the empirical calibration that would make it a tested equality is Open Problem 7.2. The multi-event discipline that gives empirical mode its falsifiable content is Proposition 3.8 (§3.6).

### 3.3. The three roles of the one-form

**Proposition 3.4 (One one-form, three ledger projections).** In a boundary-layer-admissible nested pair, the spin one-form $\omega_{S_i}$ carries three corpus roles, and they are facets of the same object rather than three claims about three objects:

1. *Re-authoring* the lower substrate's recurrence — the non-gradient component the upper substrate imposes (XIV essay §3 imposed-spin claim);
2. *Carrying the identity witness* $[\eta_{S_i}] \in H^1(M^{\mathrm{carrier}}_{S_i})$ — the de Rham class of the *closed component* of $\omega_{S_i}$ (equal to $[\omega_{S_i}]$ only when $\omega_{S_i}$ is globally closed) — on the lower carrier, read by $\mathcal{T}$ (XIV companion §11.1) over the recurrence cycles;
3. *Realising the maintenance cost* of nesting, $M^{\mathrm{cost}}_{S_i}(\nu, e) = c \cdot |\sigma^{(M)}_{S_i}(\nu, e)|$ (Theorem 2), where $\sigma^{(M)}$ pairs the declared envelope realisation of the spin field with the boundary cycle in the upper-state-space neighbourhood (Lemma 3.3, Remark 3.3a).

*Proof.* All three are readings of the single spin field $\omega_{S_i}$ (and its declared envelope realisation): role 1 is the form acting on the dynamics; role 2 is its pairing with the recurrence cycles on the lower carrier; role 3 is the realisation's pairing with the boundary cycle in the upper neighbourhood (Lemma 3.3). They project onto distinct ledger lines — role 1 onto the dynamics, role 2 onto the witness functor, role 3 onto the X+M maintenance ledger — reading the *same field* against *different $1$-cycles in their respective loci*, not a single cohomology class shared across loci (Remark 3.3a). $\square$

**[ALIGNMENT]** *Reconciliation with the two-channel reading.* Operational Spin's two-channel decomposition separates the closed (homological) channel carrying $[\eta_{S_i}]$ from the operational-spin (exact, $d\omega \neq 0$) channel; XIV v6.1.1 named roles 1 and 2 of Proposition 3.4 within that decomposition. Theorem 2 names role 3 — the maintenance-cost carrier — which is invisible at the two-layer scope of XIV because there is no recursive maintenance overhead to attribute, and which becomes load-bearing once $M^{\mathrm{cost}}$ is given its geometric realisation. The third role is the same field paired over a different cycle — the boundary, in the upper-state-space neighbourhood — not a third channel and not the same cohomology class as the recurrence witness (Remark 3.3a): the spin field $\omega_{S_i}$ is one object, read against the recurrence cycles (role 2, lower carrier) and the boundary cycle (role 3, upper neighbourhood).

### 3.5. The boundary-witness-compatible subclass (Proposition 3.7)

Remark 3.3a records that, in the general boundary-layer-admissible class, the maintenance-circulation $\sigma^{(M)}$ and the recurrence witness $[\eta_{S_i}]$ are two pairings of the same spin field over two cycles in two loci, with no a-priori relation between the loci. There is a declared subclass on which such a relation is supplied — and on it the *net-circulation* $\sigma^{(M)}$ ceases to be a free declared datum and is *evaluated* as the transported witness period. The device is a declared map from the upper envelope to the lower carrier.

**Definition 3.5 (Boundary-witness-compatible subclass).** A boundary-layer-admissible nested pair (Definition 3.2), read in *net-circulation* mode, is *boundary-witness-compatible* iff the deployment declares and seals, in addition to the data of Definition 3.2, a single map $\rho \colon U_{\nu, e} \to M^{\mathrm{carrier}}_{S_i}$ (a graph map in the finite-state reading, a smooth map in the continuous reading) satisfying:
- **(H-a)** $\rho_*[\Gamma_{\nu, e}] = [\gamma_i]$ in $H_1(M^{\mathrm{carrier}}_{S_i})$, as *oriented, degree-one* classes, where $\gamma_i$ is the recurrence cycle carrying the witness — $\langle [\eta_{S_i}], [\gamma_i] \rangle \neq 0$, a defining requirement of the subclass, enforced alongside the two equalities;
- **(H-b)** $[\widetilde{\omega}^{\nu, e}_{S_i}] = \rho^*[\eta_{S_i}]$ in $H^1(U_{\nu, e})$ — the declared envelope realisation is the $\rho$-pullback of the witness's closed component.

**Lemma 3.6 (Induced-map duality).** For the single map $\rho$ of Definition 3.5, inducing $\rho_* \colon H_1(U_{\nu, e}) \to H_1(M^{\mathrm{carrier}}_{S_i})$ and $\rho^* \colon H^1(M^{\mathrm{carrier}}_{S_i}) \to H^1(U_{\nu, e})$, one has
$$\langle \rho^* \alpha, z \rangle_{U} = \langle \alpha, \rho_* z \rangle_{M} \qquad \text{for all } \alpha \in H^1(M^{\mathrm{carrier}}_{S_i}),\ z \in H_1(U_{\nu, e}).$$

*Proof.* $\rho^*$ on cochains is by definition the transpose of $\rho_*$ on chains under the evaluation pairing, and the identity descends to (co)homology because a chain map commutes with $\partial$ / $d$. It is load-bearing that $\rho_*$ and $\rho^*$ are induced by *one and the same* $\rho$: two *independently* declared maps $H_1(U) \to H_1(M)$ and $H^1(M) \to H^1(U)$ need not be transpose, and the duality can then fail. $\square$

**Proposition 3.7 (Boundary–Witness Compatibility).** On the boundary-witness-compatible subclass, in net-circulation mode, the maintenance-circulation equals the transported witness period:
$$\sigma^{(M)}_{S_i}(\nu, e) = \langle [\widetilde{\omega}^{\nu, e}_{S_i}], [\Gamma_{\nu, e}] \rangle = \langle [\eta_{S_i}], [\gamma_i] \rangle.$$

*Proof.* $\sigma^{(M)}_{S_i}(\nu, e) = \langle [\widetilde{\omega}^{\nu, e}_{S_i}], [\Gamma_{\nu, e}] \rangle$ (net-circulation mode is a period, Lemma 3.3) $= \langle \rho^*[\eta_{S_i}], [\Gamma_{\nu, e}] \rangle$ (H-b) $= \langle [\eta_{S_i}], \rho_*[\Gamma_{\nu, e}] \rangle$ (Lemma 3.6) $= \langle [\eta_{S_i}], [\gamma_i] \rangle$ (H-a). $\blacksquare$

**Register (what is proven vs. what stays declared).** Proposition 3.7 does *not* upgrade the Bridge Law from declared to derived. The maintenance-per-circulation-unit constant $c$ and the proportionality $M^{\mathrm{cost}} = c \cdot |\sigma^{(M)}|$ (Theorem 2) remain a declared structural law; and the compatibility data $(\rho, \text{H-a}, \text{H-b})$ is itself *declared and sealed*, not derived. What the proposition delivers is that, *conditional on that declaration* and *in net-circulation mode only*, the previously-free scalar $\sigma^{(M)}$ is forced to equal the lower witness period — the raw-$\sigma$ declaration is *relocated* into the more explicit, falsifiable, cohomological declaration H-b, not removed. Three hypotheses are load-bearing, and each fails a concrete instance: H-a's *degree-one orientation* (a degree-$m$ map gives $\sigma^{(M)} = m \cdot \langle [\eta_{S_i}], [\gamma_i] \rangle$, not the witness period, and a map collapsing $[\Gamma_{\nu, e}]$ to $0$ fails H-a outright, landing outside the subclass); H-b's *cohomology* equality (not mere pointwise agreement of representatives); and the *net-circulation* mode (the norm and vorticity-flux functionals are not period pairings, so Lemma 3.6 does not apply — on the compatible subclass they stay declared). Outside the compatible subclass — the norm/flux functionals, and the general boundary-layer-admissible class — the maintenance geometry retains the declared-structural-law register of Theorem 2.

**Consistency with Remark 3.3a.** Proposition 3.7 does not identify the two loci. $[\widetilde{\omega}^{\nu, e}_{S_i}]$ and $[\eta_{S_i}]$ remain classes on different spaces ($U_{\nu, e}$ and $M^{\mathrm{carrier}}_{S_i}$); they are *related through the declared* $\rho^*$, and their periods coincide only *after transport* across the loci. The "one field, two loci, two pairings, not one class" reading of Remark 3.3a stands unchanged for the general class; the compatible subclass adds a declared morphism $\rho$ under which the upper period is the image of the lower witness period, without collapsing the two loci into one shared class.

**Finite verifiability.** For declared finite-graph data, membership in the boundary-witness-compatible subclass is decidable in polynomial time: (H-a) is a pushforward and a coefficient comparison in the fundamental-cycle basis, (H-b) a comparison of periods on that basis, and non-contractibility of $\Gamma_{\nu, e}$ a Betti-number and class computation — all finite linear algebra over $\mathbb{Q}$. This is the formal content of the algebraic half of Open Problem 7.7; what remains open there is the deployment-side certification (of $\rho$ and of the realisation rule) and the general-class loci question, not the algebra.

### 3.6. The empirical-mode falsifier

**Proposition 3.8 (Multi-event $c$-consistency falsifier).** In empirical mode (§3.2) a *single* event with $|\sigma^{(M)}| > 0$ carries no falsifiable content against an *unsealed* constant: the choice $c := M^{\mathrm{cost}} / |\sigma^{(M)}|$ fits it exactly. The empirical content of the declared proportionality is therefore class-level. Fix a declared class read in *norm* mode — the clean zero-reading mode: under the vorticity-flux a measured zero signals an *inadmissible declaration* (the flux mode requires a non-closed realisation on $\Sigma$, Definitions 3.1–3.2) and the event is excluded before scoring, while a net-circulation zero may be spurious cancellation — with tolerance $\varepsilon \ge 0$ and measured pairs $(M_j, |\sigma_j|)$ on events $e_1, \dots, e_n$:

(a) *(Sealed-$c$ test, $n \ge 1$.)* Under a constant $c > 0$ sealed before the events are scored, the law holds on the set iff $\bigl| M_j - c\,|\sigma_j| \bigr| \le \varepsilon$ for every $j$. Even a single event can violate this.

(b) *(Free-$c$ class falsifier, $n \ge 2$.)* The law is *falsified for the class* iff no $c > 0$ satisfies the bound of (a) for all $j$ — equivalently, iff some event has $|\sigma_j| = 0$ with $M_j > \varepsilon$ (clause (c)), or the per-event feasible intervals $\bigl[ (M_j - \varepsilon)/|\sigma_j|,\ (M_j + \varepsilon)/|\sigma_j| \bigr]$ over the events with $|\sigma_j| > 0$ have empty intersection in $(0, \infty)$. An event set that does not constrain $c$ at all — every $|\sigma_j| = 0$ with $M_j \le \varepsilon$ — is *not falsified* (every $c > 0$ fits it); it is simply not a test of the proportionality.

(c) *(Zero-spin control, norm mode.)* An event with $|\sigma_j| = 0$ but $M_j > \varepsilon$ falsifies outright, since $c \cdot 0 = 0$ cannot reach $M_j$; an event with $|\sigma_j| = 0$ and $M_j \le \varepsilon$ constrains nothing.

**Discipline (what keeps this a test rather than a curve-fit).** Two admissible protocols. *Fully sealed:* $c$ is declared before any cross-layer event observation — the sealing of XIV companion Definition 2.6, and the $c$ of the boundary-layer-admissible declaration (Definition 3.2); clause (a) then applies from the first event. *Calibration-sealed:* $c$ is fitted on a calibration event set, frozen, and validated only on a *holdout* set disjoint from the calibration events — $c$ is never scored against the events that fitted it (a strictly weaker discipline than Definition 2.6's, disclosed as such; the class declaration completes at the freeze, before any scored event, which is how Definition 3.2's seal reads under this protocol). Under either protocol the constant is *shared across the declared class*: without the shared-$c$ requirement, XV.2 would be a re-parameterisation of $M^{\mathrm{cost}}$ event by event, not a falsifiable proportionality — this proposition is what gives the declared Bridge Law empirical teeth in empirical mode. The same discipline binds the instrument: the envelope realisation and the locus selections are governed by rules fixed for the class (Definition 3.2), so the readings $|\sigma_j|$ are produced under a fixed instrument discipline rather than re-declared event by event. The maintenance readings $M_j$ are bound by the same sealing: they are scored *net of the declared spin-independent baseline* (the sealed reference protocol of §3.2's empirical mode), so clause (c)'s zero-spin control tests the spin-attributed residual — background protocol overhead cannot produce a spurious falsification, while a residual maintenance reading at a zero spin reading still falsifies the class attribution outright. (Calibration of the *value* of $c$ remains Open Problem 7.2; Proposition 3.8 concerns the *consistency* of one constant across events, which clause (b) tests without knowing $c$ in advance.)

### 3.7. Refinements of the declared law

**Definition 3.9 (Primitive-normalized witness).** The witness **class** $[\eta_{S_i}]$ (presupposed non-trivial) is *primitive-normalized* under a declared unit choice when its period on a declared *primitive* recurrence cycle $\gamma_{\mathrm{prim}}$ — a generator of the homology summand carrying the witness; a fundamental cycle in the finite-state reading — equals $\pm 1$: $\langle [\eta_{S_i}], \gamma_{\mathrm{prim}} \rangle = \pm 1$. This is a property of the class and its declared scale, not of a representative: periods are class invariants (every representative has the same periods, Lemma 3.3's carrier-side analogue), so normalization is achieved by rescaling the field — a unit declaration $\eta \mapsto \lambda \eta$, sealed with the class data (XIV companion Definition 2.6) — never by choosing a representative. Achievability over $\mathbb{Q}$ (or $\mathbb{R}$) needs only $[\eta_{S_i}] \neq 0$: non-degeneracy of the pairing supplies a generator with non-zero period.

What it buys — *scoped honestly*. In the general boundary-layer-admissible class the envelope realisation $\widetilde{\omega}$ is a separately declared datum, and the deployment retains the rescaling freedom $\widetilde{\omega} \mapsto \lambda \widetilde{\omega}$, $c \mapsto c/\lambda$ verbatim, normalized witness or not. On the *boundary-witness-compatible subclass in net-circulation mode*, however, (H-a) and (H-b) *together* tie the envelope period to the witness: $\sigma^{(M)} = \langle [\eta_{S_i}], [\gamma_i] \rangle$ (Proposition 3.7), which under this normalization equals $\pm 1$ exactly when $[\gamma_i]$ agrees with $\pm[\gamma_{\mathrm{prim}}]$ up to an element of the pairing kernel of $[\eta_{S_i}]$ — in particular when $[\gamma_i] = \pm[\gamma_{\mathrm{prim}}]$ (and $\pm k$ when $[\gamma_i] = k[\gamma_{\mathrm{prim}}]$ modulo that kernel). There the scale freedom is fixed and $c$ reads as *maintenance per witness-unit*: the multi-event consistency test (Proposition 3.8) compares like with like across events and classes, and the calibration protocol of Open Problem 7.2 has one constant to pin rather than a scale-orbit of them.

**Remark 3.10 (Two-channel refinement of the Bridge Law — declared).** The declared boundary functionals of Definition 3.1 split into two *channels* of the one field. The **homological channel** is the net-circulation period $\sigma_H = \langle [\widetilde{\omega}^{\nu, e}_{S_i}], [\Gamma_{\nu, e}] \rangle$ — gauge-invariant (Lemma 3.3), class-determined, and on the compatible subclass equal to the transported witness period (Proposition 3.7). The **local channel** is the magnitude reading $\Lambda_{\mathrm{loc}}$ with its sealed measure datum: the *norm* functional — gauge-**sensitive** (it moves under $\widetilde{\omega} \mapsto \widetilde{\omega} + df$) and blind to homology — or the *vorticity-flux* functional, which is gauge-**invariant** ($d(\widetilde{\omega} + df) = d\widetilde{\omega}$) yet still class-blind. Theorem 2's register already licenses richer declared laws; the two-channel form
$$
M^{\mathrm{cost}}_{S_i}(\nu, e) \;=\; c_H \cdot \bigl| \sigma_H \bigr| \;+\; c_L \cdot \Lambda_{\mathrm{loc}},
$$
with both constants declared and sealed, is the canonical such refinement, *typed as a joint declaration*: two functionals with their per-functional regularity requirements and two constants over ONE sealed realisation. The canonical pairing is (net-circulation, norm) — a single realisation closed near $\Gamma$ serves both, since the norm imposes no closedness requirement; the (net-circulation, flux) pairing is jointly declarable only with $\Sigma$ outside the closedness neighbourhood $U$ (closed on $U$, non-closed on $\Sigma$ — impossible where $\Sigma \subseteq U$). The single-functional laws are the $c_L = 0$ (resp. $c_H = 0$) degenerations, and *those* are directly subject to the multi-event consistency discipline of Proposition 3.8; when both channels are live, the measured $M_j$ is a sum and the falsifier becomes a *two-parameter* feasibility test in $(c_H, c_L)$, developed as Proposition 3.15: two independent-reading events falsify only through the positivity constraint, and genuine overdetermination begins at three events (clause (c) there).

The refinement carries a sharp Regime-W signature, read *at the carrier level*: a kernel-annihilating transport (Theorem 3, Proposition 4.3) kills the carrier-level homological reading — the transported witness pairing is $0$ — while the carrier-level magnitude reading of the field can remain non-zero (the degree-zero instance of §4: the transported pairing dies while the target field's norm reading stays non-zero). These are the carrier-side *analogues* of the two envelope channels; they transfer to $\sigma_H$ / $\Lambda_{\mathrm{loc}}$ themselves only where a post-event compatibility declaration of the Definition 3.5 type links the envelope readings to the carrier — the two-loci discipline of Remark 3.3a governs the channels exactly as it governs the witness. The qualitative signature stands: a monitor watching only magnitude readings reports live structure on a pair whose witness is already annihilated — the magnitude-only invisibility of Regime W (XIV companion Theorem 10), now visible inside the maintenance ledger's own channel split: identity death and magnitude survival are different channels of one field, not a contradiction.

**Proposition 3.11 (Maintenance quantisation and the positive floor).** Work on the boundary-witness-compatible subclass in net-circulation mode (Definition 3.5, Proposition 3.7), and declare the witness data *integral* — the class has integer periods on all integral $1$-cycles (in the finite-state reading, an integer-valued cochain representative) — with $\gamma_i$ an integral cycle (automatic in the finite-state reading, where recurrence cycles are integer chains). Integrality is the load-bearing hypothesis; primitive normalization (Definition 3.9) is *not* needed for the statement — it fixes the *unit reading* of the quantum ($c$ as maintenance per witness-unit) and the cross-class comparability of the lattice. Then
$$
\sigma^{(M)}_{S_i}(\nu, e) \;=\; \langle [\eta_{S_i}], [\gamma_i] \rangle \;\in\; \mathbb{Z} \setminus \{0\},
$$
so $|\sigma^{(M)}| \ge 1$ and the Bridge Law's cost is *quantised with a positive floor*:
$$
M^{\mathrm{cost}}_{S_i}(\nu, e) \;=\; c \cdot |\sigma^{(M)}| \;\in\; \{c,\, 2c,\, 3c,\, \dots\} , \qquad M^{\mathrm{cost}} \ge c > 0 .
$$
**No witness-bearing compatible nesting is maintenance-free**: carrying a live witness through an event costs at least one maintenance quantum. *Proof.* Integrality gives $\sigma^{(M)} = \langle [\eta_{S_i}], [\gamma_i] \rangle \in \mathbb{Z}$ (Proposition 3.7); the witness-bearing condition of Definition 3.5 gives $\sigma^{(M)} \neq 0$; a non-zero integer has absolute value $\ge 1$. $\square$

*Corollary (M-only lattice falsifier).* Under these declarations every true cost lies on the lattice $c \cdot \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 1}$, so a measured cost $M_j$ falsifies the declaration set whenever its distance to the lattice exceeds the tolerance — **with no $\sigma$-measurement at all**; in particular any $M_j < c - \varepsilon$ (below the floor) falsifies outright. The lattice (integer-spacing) discrimination has power only for $\varepsilon < c/2$; at $\varepsilon \ge c/2$ every reading above the floor is within tolerance of the lattice and only the floor clause retains power (a maintenance-free reading still falsifies for any $\varepsilon < c$). The test's value is its *independence*: it runs where no circulation data exists at all; where $\sigma$-measurements are available, the sealed-$c$ test of Proposition 3.8(a) subsumes it (integer $\sigma_j$ plus per-event consistency implies lattice membership), and the two tests are incomparable in the free-$c$ reading.

**[ALIGNMENT]** *The price floor.* Proposition 3.11 is the provable instance, on the compatible subclass, of the maintenance-ledger thesis of *Why the Ocean Has No Chance* §III: where the Bridge Law and compatible integral witness data are declared and the witness is genuinely carried, the maintenance ledger line is *provably* bounded below by a quantum — within those declarations, no bookkeeping choice can zero it out.

**Lemma 3.12 (Channel inequality).** On the same declared locus (the envelope cycle $\Gamma_{\nu, e}$, realisation $\widetilde{\omega}$), with the counting measure — and any measure datum $w \ge 1$ —
$$
\Lambda_{\mathrm{loc}}(\Gamma) \;=\; \sum_e |\widetilde{\omega}(e)|\,|z(e)|\,w(e) \;\ge\; \Bigl| \sum_e \widetilde{\omega}(e)\, z(e) \Bigr| \;=\; |\sigma_H| ,
$$
with equality (for $w \equiv 1$) iff all *non-zero* signed contributions $\widetilde{\omega}(e) z(e)$ share one sign. *Proof.* Triangle inequality. $\square$ The display is the **norm** reading of $\Lambda_{\mathrm{loc}}$; the flux reading is *not* bounded below by the period (a nearly-closed realisation on $\Sigma$ has arbitrarily small flux with a large period). Corollary: $\sigma_H \neq 0 \Rightarrow \Lambda_{\mathrm{loc}} > 0$ — a live homological channel *forces* a live norm channel — while the converse fails on the same locus: the alternating realisation on a $4$-cycle ($+1, -1, +1, -1$) has $\sigma_H = 0$ and $\Lambda_{\mathrm{loc}} = 4$. The one-directionality of the two-channel signature (Remark 3.10) is thus a theorem: local liveness never certifies a live homological (envelope) reading, but a live homological reading always leaves a local trace. Proposition 3.14 sharpens the inequality to a variational floor with exact minimisers.

**Proposition 3.13 (Harmonic realisation minimises the $L^2$-magnitude).** Finite-state reading; fix a weighted inner product $\langle \alpha, \beta \rangle_w = \sum_e w(e)\, \alpha(e)\, \beta(e)$, $w > 0$, on $1$-cochains of the envelope graph. Every cochain splits uniquely as $\widetilde{\omega} = \delta f + h$ with $h$ orthogonal to every coboundary (equivalently: the weighted divergence of $h$ vanishes at every vertex); $h$ has the same periods as $\widetilde{\omega}$, and **among all realisations $\widetilde{\omega} + \delta g$ in the class, $h$ uniquely minimises the $L^2$-magnitude $\| \cdot \|_w$**. *Proof.* Orthogonal projection onto an affine subspace: $\| h + \delta g \|_w^2 = \| h \|_w^2 + \| \delta g \|_w^2$. $\square$ The cheapest global field-magnitude compatible with a fixed homological reading is the divergence-free one; for the counting measure, Cauchy–Schwarz gives $|\sigma_H| \le \| \widetilde{\omega} \|_2 \cdot \| z \|_2$ — the $L^2$ form of Lemma 3.12 (for a general weight the correct pairing bound is $|\langle \widetilde{\omega}, z \rangle_w| \le \| \widetilde{\omega} \|_w \| z \|_w$, with the *unweighted* period bounded by $\| \widetilde{\omega} \|_w \| z \|_{1/w}$). *Honest scope:* the declared norm functional of Definition 3.1 is an $\ell^1$-reading along $\Gamma$, and the harmonic representative need *not* minimise it; the variational statement is specific to the declared $L^2$ magnitude.

**Proposition 3.14 (Minimal-entrainment bound — the derived floor).** Finite-state reading, on a declared locus of Definition 3.2: the cycle $\Gamma_{\nu, e}$ with cycle-vector $z$, the sealed measure $w > 0$, and a required net-circulation period $\sigma_H = \sigma \neq 0$. Among *all* realisations $\widetilde{\omega}$ on the locus carrying that period, $\sum_e \widetilde{\omega}(e)\, z(e) = \sigma$:

(i) *(norm reading.)* $\min \Lambda_{\mathrm{loc}} = \min \sum_{e} w(e)\,|z(e)|\,|\widetilde{\omega}(e)| = c_1 \cdot |\sigma|$, with $c_1 = \min_{e \in \Gamma} w(e)$ — valid for arbitrary integer multiplicities $z(e)$; the minimum is attained by concentrating the period on a single cheapest-measure edge ($\widetilde{\omega}(e_\ast) = \sigma / z(e_\ast)$ on an edge with $w(e_\ast) = c_1$), single-signed.

(ii) *(quadratic reading.)* $\min \sum_{e} w(e)\,\widetilde{\omega}(e)^2 = \sigma^2 / L_w$, with $L_w = \sum_{e \in \Gamma} z(e)^2 / w(e)$; the minimum is attained *uniquely* by the $w$-harmonic profile $\widetilde{\omega} = \sigma\, z / (w\, L_w)$ — the cycle reading of Proposition 3.13's minimiser (the harmonic representative of the class it itself defines; on a $b_1 = 1$ locus the period constraint fixes the class, so this quadratic problem coincides with Proposition 3.13's class-constrained minimisation — the $\ell^1$ problem of (i) keeps its distinct, concentrated minimiser).

*Proof.* (i) $c_1 |\sigma| = c_1 \bigl|\sum \widetilde{\omega} z\bigr| \le c_1 \sum |\widetilde{\omega}|\,|z| \le \sum w(e)\,|z(e)|\,|\widetilde{\omega}(e)| = \Lambda_{\mathrm{loc}}$ — the triangle inequality that powers Lemma 3.12 — and the concentrated realisation contributes $|\sigma / z(e_\ast)| \cdot |z(e_\ast)| \cdot w(e_\ast) = c_1 |\sigma|$, meeting the bound with equality. (ii) Weighted Cauchy–Schwarz: $\sigma^2 = \bigl(\sum (\sqrt{w}\,\widetilde{\omega})(z/\sqrt{w})\bigr)^2 \le \bigl(\sum w \widetilde{\omega}^2\bigr) \cdot L_w$, with equality iff $\widetilde{\omega} \propto z/w$; the period constraint fixes the proportionality constant to $\sigma/L_w$, and strict convexity gives uniqueness. $\square$

Its unconditional content: no realisation can carry a non-trivial period with an arbitrarily small field — the norm and quadratic readings of *any* period-$\sigma$ realisation are bounded below by constants computed from the declared locus geometry $(\Gamma, w)$ alone (the vorticity-flux reading is *not* — Lemma 3.12's exception stands), and with integral witness data (Proposition 3.11, $|\sigma| \ge 1$) every witness-bearing compatible nesting has $\Lambda_{\mathrm{loc}} \ge c_1 > 0$ — a *derived* positive floor on the field reading, alongside the declared cost floor of Proposition 3.11. *Corollary (floor gate, fully derived).* A measured pair of readings $(\Lambda_{\mathrm{loc}}, \sigma_H)$ *of one realisation on one declared locus* with $\Lambda_{\mathrm{loc}} < c_1 |\sigma_H|$ is impossible: no realisation whatever produces it. **Proposition 3.15 (Two-channel feasibility falsifier).** In empirical mode, fix the two-channel declared law of Remark 3.10, $M^{\mathrm{cost}} = c_H |\sigma_H| + c_L \Lambda_{\mathrm{loc}}$, with both constants sealed and $\ge 0$ (the degenerations $c_L = 0$ / $c_H = 0$ being the single-functional laws), a tolerance $\varepsilon \ge 0$, and measured triples $(M_j, |\sigma_{H,j}|, \Lambda_j)$ on events $e_1, \dots, e_n$. (c) *(Power structure.)* Two events whose reading vectors $(|\sigma_H|, \Lambda)$ are linearly independent determine a unique exact solve; when that solve is non-negative the two events are fittable exactly and never falsify, for any $\varepsilon$ — two non-parallel bands always intersect, so a two-event falsification can arise only from the positivity constraint (an exact solve outside the quadrant, at small tolerance). With dependent reading vectors the test reduces to the single-channel discipline of Proposition 3.8 — verdict-exact under clause (d)'s agreement condition, and inheriting the same degenerate corner. Genuine overdetermination therefore begins at three events — the single-event unfalsifiability of Proposition 3.8, lifted one dimension up.

## 4. Core-Reduction Annihilates Witness (XV.3)

There is a tempting move, watching an upper substrate carry a lower one, to say the lower substrate has simply *become part of* the carrier — that the transport unit at the next level up is the carrier's body and the lower identity disappears into the carrying. The move is tempting as a description of motion, but it is false at the witness level, and its falsity is a theorem of general topology applied to the imported carrier structure.

**Definition 4.1 (Witness-preserving transport; TopWit-honest transport).** A transport scheme on the lower substrate of a nested pair is realised by a carrier-map $\phi$ between identity-supporting carriers, inducing $\phi^* \colon H^1(M') \to H^1(M^{\mathrm{carrier}}_{S_i})$ on first cohomology (equivalently $\phi_* \colon H_1(M^{\mathrm{carrier}}_{S_i}) \to H_1(M')$ on homology). Following Operational Spin, $\phi$ is *witness-preserving* iff it preserves the non-vanishing of the witness pairing: for every recurrent class $[\gamma]$ with $\langle [\eta_{S_i}], [\gamma] \rangle \neq 0$ one has $\langle [\eta'], \phi_*[\gamma] \rangle \neq 0$. This *liveness* condition is deliberately weaker than the morphism condition of the imported category $\mathsf{TopWit}$ (§1.3), which is *class-exact*: $\phi^*[\eta'] = [\eta_{S_i}]$. Class-exactness implies liveness on every witness-bearing class ($\langle [\eta'], \phi_*[\gamma] \rangle = \langle \phi^*[\eta'], [\gamma] \rangle = \langle [\eta_{S_i}], [\gamma] \rangle \neq 0$); the converse fails — a doubling with $\phi^*[\eta'] = 2[\eta_{S_i}]$ preserves liveness on every class while breaking class-exactness — so the two notions are named apart wherever the distinction is load-bearing. A transport is *TopWit-honest* iff it is witness-preserving *and* does not factor through a contractible carrier. The honest transport unit is the pair $(M^{\mathrm{carrier}}_{S_i}, [\eta_{S_i}])$ taken together — the carrier as the necessary substrate of the witness class.

**Theorem 3 (Core-Reduction Annihilates Witness).** Let $(\mathfrak{S}, \mathfrak{S}_i, \nu)$ be a nested pair with identity-supporting carrier $M^{\mathrm{carrier}}_{S_i}$ and witness $[\eta_{S_i}] \in H^1(M^{\mathrm{carrier}}_{S_i})$, and let a transport scheme's carrier-map factor through a *contractible core* — a point, a tree, or any contractible factoring object $K$ with $H^1(K) = 0$. Then: **(i) non-preservation** — the induced map on first cohomology is the zero map, so *no* such transport can preserve a non-trivial witness. **(ii) annihilation** — *if the transport is moreover a* core-reduction*, i.e. its post-event carrier is the contractible core $K$ itself (or deformation-retracts to it), as in the folding case below*, then the post-transport witness lives in $H^1(K) = 0$ and is annihilated, $[\eta^{(\text{post})}_{S_i}] = 0$. (When the post-event carrier is some $M'$ that merely *receives* a transport factoring through $K$, only (i) holds; $H^1(M')$ may be non-zero, but the source witness is still not carried across.)

**Proof.** Write the transport carrier-map as a factorisation $\phi = g \circ f$ with $f \colon M^{\mathrm{carrier}}_{S_i} \to K$, $g \colon K \to M'$, and $K$ contractible. By homotopy invariance of cohomology (de Rham case: the Poincaré lemma; graph case: a tree has empty cycle space), $H^1(K) = 0$. The induced map factors as
$$
\phi^* \;=\; f^* \circ g^* \;\colon\; H^1(M') \xrightarrow{\;g^*\;} H^1(K) = 0 \xrightarrow{\;f^*\;} H^1(M^{\mathrm{carrier}}_{S_i}),
$$
a composite through the zero group, hence the zero map. (The cohomology reading $\phi^*$ and the homology reading $\phi_*$ agree *at the zero level* by the non-degeneracy of the de Rham pairing $H^1(M; \mathbb{R}) \times H_1(M; \mathbb{R}) \to \mathbb{R}$ over the reals — the pairing Operational Spin uses: each induced map is the transpose of the other under the pairing, so $\phi^* = 0$ iff $\phi_* = 0$, and the class-exact preservation of §1.3 and the liveness preservation of Definition 4.1 therefore fail *together* here. In general the two notions differ — Definition 4.1's note — but the present argument needs only the zero level.) Two distinct conclusions follow. *(i) Impossibility:* the $\mathsf{TopWit}$ witness-preservation equation requires $\phi^*[\eta'] = [\eta_{S_i}]$ for the transported target witness $[\eta']$; but $\phi^* = 0$ forces $[\eta_{S_i}] = \phi^*[\eta'] = 0$, so no transport with a contractible factor can preserve a non-trivial source witness $[\eta_{S_i}] \neq 0$ (equivalently, on homology $\phi_*[\gamma] = 0$ for every recurrent $[\gamma]$, so the pairing $\langle [\eta'], \phi_*[\gamma] \rangle$ vanishes identically and $\phi$ is not witness-preserving, Definition 4.1). *(ii) Annihilation* (the core-reduction case, post-event carrier $= K$ contractible): the post-transport witness lives in $H^1(K) = 0$, so it is identically zero, $[\eta^{(\mathrm{post})}_{S_i}] = 0$. It is conclusion (ii) — which *requires* the contractible post-carrier — that supplies condition (4) of Regime W (XIV companion Definition 11.4); the folding witness (Proposition 4.2) is the case where the post-carrier is exactly the contractible image. $\blacksquare$

**Proposition 4.2 (The folding of Theorem 10 is a core-reduction).** The folding map $\tau \colon S^1 \times \mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{R}$, $(\theta, y) \mapsto y$, of the XIV companion Theorem 10 (imported from Operational Spin) is a core-reduction transport in the sense of Theorem 3: its image $\mathbb{R}$ is contractible, the pre-event witness $[d\theta] \in H^1_{\mathrm{dR}}(S^1 \times \mathbb{R})$ with winding period $2\pi$ has no non-zero target in $H^1_{\mathrm{dR}}(\mathbb{R}) = 0$, and the induced $\tau^* \colon H^1_{\mathrm{dR}}(\mathbb{R}) = 0 \to H^1_{\mathrm{dR}}(S^1 \times \mathbb{R})$ is the zero map. The winding period collapses $2\pi \to 0$; the witness is annihilated.

*Proof.* Direct application of Theorem 3 with $K = M' = \mathbb{R}$ and $f = \tau$, $g = \mathrm{id}_\mathbb{R}$. In Operational Spin's terms, $\tau$ is a viability-exact morphism that is not witness-preserving — exactly the contractible-factor case. $\square$

**Proposition 4.3 (Witness Transport Kernel Criterion).** Core-reduction (Theorem 3) is the contractible instance of a more general criterion that governs *any* transport, contractible factor or not. Let a transport carrier-map $\phi \colon M^{\mathrm{carrier}}_{S_i} \to M'$ induce $\phi_* \colon H_1(M^{\mathrm{carrier}}_{S_i}) \to H_1(M')$, and let $W := \{[\gamma] : \langle [\eta_{S_i}], [\gamma] \rangle \neq 0\}$ be the witness-bearing recurrence classes; presuppose the source witness non-trivial with at least one witness-bearing class, $[\eta_{S_i}] \neq 0$ and $W \neq \emptyset$ (otherwise every case below holds vacuously and the trichotomy carries no content). Relative to $W$ and the transported witness $[\eta']$, the transport is:

- **witness-preserving** iff $\langle [\eta'], \phi_*[\gamma] \rangle \neq 0$ for *every* $[\gamma] \in W$;
- **witness-annihilating** iff $\langle [\eta'], \phi_*[\gamma] \rangle = 0$ for *every* $[\gamma] \in W$ — i.e. $\phi_*$ sends every witness-bearing class into the *pairing kernel* of $[\eta']$;
- **partial** iff some are preserved and some annihilated.

The determining datum is the induced map $\phi_*$ *on the specific witness-bearing classes*, not the contractibility of the carrier.

*Proof.* Immediate from Definition 4.1: $\phi$ preserves the witness on $[\gamma]$ exactly when $\langle [\eta'], \phi_*[\gamma] \rangle \neq 0$, and the three cases exhaust $W$. $\square$

**Core-reduction as the contractible corollary.** When $\phi$ factors through an $H^1$-trivial (in particular contractible) carrier $K$, one has $\phi^* = 0$ (Theorem 3 proof), so $\phi_*[\gamma] = 0$ for *every* class and $\phi$ is witness-annihilating — this is the contractible-core case of Proposition 4.3, i.e. Theorem 3(i) is its corollary (Theorem 3(ii)'s outright class death uses the contractible post-carrier hypothesis, which Proposition 4.3 does not). The shared word marks two levels: Proposition 4.3's *witness-annihilating* is the pairing-level verdict — every transported reading vanishes — strictly weaker than Theorem 3(ii)'s annihilation of the post-carrier witness class itself. Contractibility is *sufficient but not necessary*: the first stage of the Remark 4.5 chain below annihilates relative to the middle witness while its transported class survives — and a contractible factor is impossible there, since one would force $\phi_* = 0$. (The degree-zero self-map of a non-contractible carrier also annihilates, killing every class with non-contractible pre- and post-carriers; its winding-zero walk does, however, lift through a finite tree — a contractible-factor instance in disguise — so what it witnesses is that the *post-carrier* need not be the core, not the absence of a contractible factor.) Core-reduction is thus the *canonical*, not the only, route into the annihilating case; the general obstruction is that $\phi_*$ lands the witness-bearing classes in the pairing kernel.

**Remark 4.4 (Witness retention index).** Proposition 4.3's set $W$ of witness-bearing classes is infinite whenever non-empty (all non-zero multiples pair non-trivially); the quantitative reading works over a **declared finite non-empty subset** $W_0 \subseteq W$ — the deployment's recurrence channels of record. The *retention index*
$$
R_\phi \;:=\; \frac{\#\{ [\gamma] \in W_0 : \langle [\eta'], \phi_*[\gamma] \rangle \neq 0 \}}{\# W_0}
$$
refines the trichotomy of Proposition 4.3 into a gradient *relative to $W_0$*: $R_\phi = 1$ (all declared channels retained), $R_\phi = 0$ (all killed), $0 < R_\phi < 1$ (partial) — a transport that keeps one of two declared recurrence channels retains exactly $R_\phi = 1/2$. $R_\phi = 1$ on $W_0$ does *not* by itself certify Definition 4.1 preservation over the full $W$; it certifies the declared channels. The binary verdict suffices at depth $2$; the depth-$n$ collapse-propagation programme (Open Problem 7.4) will need the gradient rather than the all-or-nothing verdict.

**Remark 4.5 (Composition of transports).** For a chain $\phi \colon M \to M'$, $\psi \colon M' \to M''$ one has $(\psi \circ \phi)_* = \psi_* \circ \phi_*$, and two facts of *different strength* follow. *(a) Class-kill propagates:* if $\phi_*[\gamma] = 0$ in $H_1(M')$ — as for any contractible-factor or degree-zero stage — then $(\psi \circ \phi)_*[\gamma] = 0$ and every continuation is annihilating on $[\gamma]$; once the class itself is dead, nothing downstream revives it. *(b) Pairing-kill does not propagate:* $\langle [\eta'], \phi_*[\gamma] \rangle = 0$ with $\phi_*[\gamma] \neq 0$ is a witness-*relative* reading, and a later stage can pair the surviving class non-trivially against its own witness. An explicit finite chain exhibits this: $C_3 \to$ figure-eight $\to C_2$, where the middle witness reads only the loop the image avoids (middle pairing $0$, class alive) and the composite nonetheless preserves the witness with period $2$. Annihilation verdicts therefore compose only at the *class* level; witness-relative verdicts must be re-read per stage. The full composition calculus is groundwork for depth-$n$ towers (Open Problem 7.4).

**Proposition 4.6 (Witness capacity; the bottleneck bound).** $\phi_*$ is a $\mathbb{Q}$-linear map on $H_1(\cdot; \mathbb{Q})$, whose dimension is $b_1$. For a declared finite set $W_0$ of witness-bearing classes (Remark 4.4):

(i) $\dim \phi_*\bigl(\mathrm{span}\, W_0\bigr) \le \mathrm{rank}\, \phi_* \le \min\bigl(b_1(M^{\mathrm{carrier}}_{S_i}),\, b_1(M')\bigr)$ — **any subfamily of $W_0$ whose transported images are linearly independent has at most that many members**. The bound is about the *images*: many classes can retain non-zero images that are all multiples of one generator, so non-vanishing alone certifies nothing about how much independent witness structure survives.

(ii) *(Pigeonhole.)* If $\dim \mathrm{span}\, W_0 > \mathrm{rank}\, \phi_*$, then $\mathrm{span}\, W_0 \cap \ker \phi_* \neq 0$: some non-zero rational combination of the declared witness-bearing classes is class-killed — and stays killed through every continuation (Proposition 4.7).

(iii) *(Chains.)* $\mathrm{rank}(\psi_* \circ \phi_*) \le \min$ of $b_1$ over *all* carriers in the chain, including the intermediate ones: **a transport chain's identity capacity is the smallest first Betti number it passes through**. One cannot carry three independent windings through a figure-eight.

(iv) Theorem 3 is the $b_1 = 0$ case: a factor with $b_1(K) = 0$ forces $\mathrm{rank} \le 0$, i.e. $\phi_* = 0$.

*Proof.* (i), (iii): rank inequalities for compositions of linear maps, plus $\mathrm{rank}\, \phi_* \le \min(\dim H_1(M), \dim H_1(M'))$. (ii): dimension count. (iv): specialisation. $\square$

**Proposition 4.7 (Composition calculus).** Define the *class-retention* of $\phi$ over $W_0$ as $R^{\mathrm{cls}}_\phi := \#\{ \gamma \in W_0 : \phi_*[\gamma] \neq 0 \} / \# W_0$. Then:

(a) $\{\gamma : (\psi \circ \phi)_* \gamma \neq 0\} \subseteq \{\gamma : \phi_* \gamma \neq 0\}$, so **$R^{\mathrm{cls}}$ is monotone non-increasing along chains** — classes die and never resurrect;

(b) the pairing-retention $R_\phi$ of Remark 4.4 is *not* monotone: it is witness-relative, and the Remark 4.5 chain realises a pairing-dead middle reading with a witness-preserving composite;

(c) Proposition 4.6(iii) is the quantitative envelope of (a): along a chain, the number of $W_0$-classes with independent surviving images is bounded by the bottleneck $b_1$, and $R^{\mathrm{cls}}$ can only step down as stages accumulate.

*Proof.* (a) is linearity: $\phi_*\gamma = 0 \Rightarrow \psi_*\phi_*\gamma = 0$. (b) is Remark 4.5's exhibit. (c) combines (a) with Proposition 4.6(iii). $\square$ Together, (a) and (b) are the honest calculus depth-$n$ needs: one invariant that genuinely accumulates (class survival) and one reading that must be re-evaluated per stage (witness pairing).

**Theorem 4 (Core-Reduction Executes Regime W under Preservation Conditions).** A core-reduction transport applied to the lower substrate of a nested pair as a cross-layer event $e$ delivers condition (4) of Regime W (XIV companion Definition 11.4), $[\eta^{(e)}_{S_i}] = 0$, *unconditionally* by Theorem 3. When the transport is additionally *budget- and interior-preserving* — as the folding construction of XIV companion Theorem 10 is, since it keeps the physical carrier and its dynamics unchanged — the remaining conditions (1) budget intact, (2) admissible interior non-empty, and (3) holding-field and $\sigma$-rotation prose criteria passing hold too, so the pair is in *full* Regime W under $e$, and the collapse is invisible to the magnitude-only monitor class $\mathfrak{M}_{|\cdot|}$ by Theorem 10. (A core-reduction realised differently — one that also closes the admissible interior, or spends the budget — still delivers condition (4) but may fail (1) or (2), and is then a different death-mode, not Regime W.) On the folding witness, core-reduction transport is, in cohomology, the same fact as the witness-collapse death-mode: the static reading of Regime W (a state of the lower substrate) and the dynamic reading (an action of transport) coincide.

**Proof.** Condition (4) is Theorem 3 — the only condition established *formally* here. The folding construction of XIV companion Theorem 10 supplies conditions (1)–(3): the post-event burden on the non-saturating run is unchanged (folding is geometric, not energetic), the contractible image $\mathbb{R}$ admits continuations so the admissible interior is non-empty, and the physical carrier $S^1 \times \mathbb{R}$ and its dynamics are unchanged so the magnitude-sampled holding-field and $\sigma$-rotation criteria continue to pass — conditions (1)–(3) inherited at the prose / deployment-witness level of XIV companion Definition 11.4 clause (3), not re-proved here. All four conditions of Definition 11.4 then hold; the pair is in Regime W under $e$, and Theorem 10 gives the magnitude-monitor invisibility. $\blacksquare$

**[ALIGNMENT]** *Architectural context.* Theorem 3 sits on the witness axis of XIV's death map, dual to Theorem 1 on the budget axis. Theorem 1 fails — a substrate cannot host nesting — when every genuine motion saturates the budget (the sealed core). Theorem 3 fails — a transport annihilates the witness — when the carrier admits no non-trivial cohomology to survive the transport (the contractible core). The temptation to call both kinds of object "a core" is the temptation Theorem 3 names formally: calling the transport unit "just the carrier's body" is the cohomological form of calling the sealed core "a substrate that has size". Both are terminology-by-shrinkage that does not preserve what the corpus tracks — the budget's sub-capacity values in the first case, the witness class in the second. The role / substrate distinction of ONTOΣ XIII is preserved at every depth: each level plays the role of upper relative to its inner and lower relative to its outer, without collapsing into one undifferentiated core.

### The tower layer: inter-level transport typing

Everything above types a transport at *one* level. A tower couples levels through the nesting morphisms, and the question a depth-$n$ statement needs answered first is one of typing: how does a transport at level $k$ act on the substrate nested at level $k-1$? The answer requires no new machinery — only the embedding.

**Definition 4.8 (Tower; image-preservation).** A *tower of depth $n$* is a chain of nested pairs $(\mathfrak{S}_{(k)}, \mathfrak{S}_{(k-1)}, \nu_k) \in \mathsf{NestedSub}_{\mathrm{nested}}$, $k = 1, \dots, n$. A transport at level $k$, realised on states by $\phi_k$ (with its carrier-map as in Definition 4.1), is *image-preserving at level $k-1$* iff $\phi_k(\nu_k(X_{(k-1)})) \subseteq \nu'_k(X'_{(k-1)})$ — the transported embedded image lands inside the post-tower embedded image.

**Lemma 4.9 (Derived restriction; cascade).** Under image-preservation the lower transport is *derived, not declared*: $\phi_{(k-1)} := (\nu'_k)^{-1} \circ \phi_k \circ \nu_k$ is a total, uniquely determined map on $X_{(k-1)}$, and $\phi_k \circ \nu_k = \nu'_k \circ \phi_{(k-1)}$ commutes by construction. Iterating, a single top-level map whose derived restrictions are image-preserving at every level determines the transport at *every* level of the tower; the restriction derived through the *composite* embedding agrees with the iterated per-level restrictions. *Proof.* $\nu'_k$ is injective, so each $\phi_k(\nu_k(x)) \in \nu'_k(X'_{(k-1)})$ has a unique preimage; totality is exactly image-preservation. For the cascade: per-level image-preservation implies the composite condition, and the composite and iterated restrictions agree on the nested images by injectivity of the composite embeddings. The converse fails — a map can preserve a composite image while breaking an intermediate one — and the per-level conditions are checked top-down, each being a condition on the previously derived map. $\square$

**Definition 4.10 (Carrier-coherence).** The derived $\phi_{(k-1)}$ acts on the lower substrate's *states*; the level's witness verdict needs its action on the lower's own recurrence carrier. $\phi_{(k-1)}$ is *carrier-coherent* iff it induces a graph map $M^{\mathrm{carrier}}_{(k-1)} \to M'^{\mathrm{carrier}}_{(k-1)}$ (vertex map plus edge $\to$ edge-path). In general this is a *declared* condition — a folding-type transport must declare its carrier data, exactly as at depth $2$. It is *automatic* in the edge-to-edge case: if $\phi_{(k-1)}$ is an $\mathsf{AdmDyn}$-morphism of the lower substrates, the image of a recurrent orbit is itself a closed admissible walk containing every image edge, so — in the finite-state reading in which a recurrence edge is one lying on a closed admissible walk — every image edge is again a recurrence edge. (Under a stricter recurrence-edge reading even this case is a declared condition.) When the derived map carries an edge to coincident endpoints the resolution is read off the carrier itself, not chosen: to an existing self-loop at that vertex when the recurrence carrier has one — which contributes under pushforward — and to a collapse, the empty edge-path, otherwise; the two are not interchangeable, since they act differently on the pushed-forward class. Two distinct graphs must not be conflated here: the lower's *own* recurrence carrier, where its witness lives, and the *embedded image* of its states inside the upper space; the typing runs through the states, and the induced carrier map is what Proposition 4.3 then classifies.

**Proposition 4.11 (Level independence; the pinch criterion).** Let a tower transport be image-preserving and carrier-coherent at the levels concerned.

(a) *(Realisability.)* All four combinations of the Proposition 4.3 verdicts at levels $k$ and $k-1$ — preserved/preserved, annihilated/preserved, preserved/annihilated, annihilated/annihilated — are realisable, each by a *single* transport on a finite tower. Level death propagates in neither direction by itself.

(b) *(Pinch, sufficient.)* Each witness-bearing recurrence class of level $k-1$ that the derived $(\phi_{(k-1)})_*$ sends into the pairing kernel of the transported witness is annihilated; when *every* witness-bearing class of the level is so sent — in particular, when the transport collapses the embedded image of the level's recurrence carrier to a contractible subset — the level is annihilated, whatever the verdict at level $k$.

*Proof.* (b) is Proposition 4.3 read through Lemma 4.9's derived restriction, applied per class; the pinch condition is checkable from the top-level map together with the level's declared carrier and witness data. (a) is by exhibition on finite towers in which the upper's recurrence carrier and the embedded lower image are *disjoint* in the upper space (nothing in the apparatus requires the embedded image to be upper-recurrent): the identity, the collapse of the upper cycle alone, the collapse of the embedded image alone, and the full collapse realise the four combinations, each image-preserving with the derived restrictions of Lemma 4.9. $\square$

*Scope note.* Clause (a) asserts *realisability*, not non-correlation: when the embedded image meets the upper carrier, the two verdicts can couple; the exhibition separates them by disjointness. The informal moral is exact — the death of the carrier does not kill the passenger unless the transport pinches the passenger's own witness-bearing cycle.

**Remark 4.12 (Additive tower floors).** Where each adjacent pair carries a declared locus $(\Gamma_k, w_k)$ with period $\sigma_k$, the per-level floors of Proposition 3.14 sum: $\sum_k \Lambda^{(k)}_{\mathrm{loc}} \ge \sum_k c_1^{(k)} |\sigma_k|$, and when each pair is moreover boundary-witness-compatible in net-circulation mode with integral witness data (the hypotheses of Proposition 3.11, so that $|\sigma_k| \ge 1$), the total is bounded below by $\sum_k c_1^{(k)}$. This is a sum of independent per-level bounds on *different loci* — it carries no cross-level content, and is recorded only as the honest tower form of the price floor.

**Proposition 4.13 (Period ladder).** Let every adjacent pair of the tower be boundary-witness-compatible (Definition 3.5, net-circulation mode).

(i) *(Parallel equalities.)* For each $k$, the pair's maintenance-circulation equals its own lower witness period: $\sigma_k = \langle [\eta_{(k-1)}], [\gamma_{(k-1)}] \rangle$ (Proposition 3.7 applied per level). These are *parallel* facts, one per pair, not a composed chain: Proposition 3.7 relates the level-$k$ *envelope* to the level-$(k-1)$ *witness*, never one level's witness to another's.

(ii) *(Composition under declared alignment.)* The level-$k$ witness $[\eta_{(k)}]$ and the envelope realisation $[\widetilde{\omega}_k]$ live on *different spaces* (the level's own carrier; a neighbourhood in the level's state space), so no class equality between them is even typed. The honest alignment is at *period* level: the deployment declares, at each intermediate level, $\langle [\eta_{(k)}], [\gamma_{(k)}] \rangle = \langle [\widetilde{\omega}_k], [\Gamma_k] \rangle$ — the level's own witness period equals the envelope period it carries for the level below. Under that declaration the per-pair equalities chain, $\sigma_{k+1} = \langle [\eta_{(k)}], [\gamma_{(k)}] \rangle = \sigma_k$, so all the maintenance-circulations coincide and the top-level circulation reads the bottom level's witness period. The alignment is a *declaration*, in the register of Definition 3.5's compatibility data, and is stated as such.

*Proof.* (i) is Proposition 3.7 per pair. (ii): for each intermediate level, $\sigma_{k+1} = \langle [\eta_{(k)}], [\gamma_{(k)}] \rangle$ by (i) applied to the pair above, and the declared period equality hands this to $\langle [\widetilde{\omega}_k], [\Gamma_k] \rangle = \sigma_k$; induction down the tower. $\square$ Whether an *undeclared* cross-level rank bound holds — a pigeonhole confining how many levels' witnesses survive one composite event through a common bottleneck — is not settled here and is recorded in Open Problem 7.4's residue.

## 5. Two-Axis Symmetry

XIV's death map carries the three collapse regimes — Drain and Snap-B visible to budget accounting, Snap-S closing the admissible interior through the state channel with the budget *stranded* rather than spent (XIV essay §9; XIV companion §7.9 measure-theoretic Drain/Snap, §10.7 spectral trichotomy, §12 dichotomy transport) — and, orthogonal to the collapse axis entirely, the witness axis carrying Regime W (XIV companion §11). XV's two eligibility theorems sit on two of these axes: Theorem 1 on the budget structure, Theorem 3 on the witness — they characterise what makes a substrate able to host a nesting at all, or a transport able to preserve its witness.

**Proposition 5.1 (Budget–witness duality).** The failure conditions of Theorem 1 (Nestability) and of Proposition 4.3 (Witness Transport Kernel Criterion) are duals along the two axes of XIV's death map. Theorem 1 concerns the *budget axis*: nestability fails exactly when every non-empty motion saturates the budget ($\Phi_S \ge C_S$), leaving no sub-capacity trajectory (sealed core). Proposition 4.3 concerns the *witness axis*: for a carrier bearing a non-trivial witness ($[\eta_{S_i}] \neq 0$), honest transport fails exactly when $\phi_*$ sends some witness-bearing recurrence class into the pairing kernel of $[\eta']$ ($\langle [\eta'], \phi_*[\gamma] \rangle = 0$) — annihilating (every such class killed) or partial (some killed); a contractible core (Theorem 3) is the canonical annihilating instance, but a degree-zero map of a non-contractible carrier fails identically. Each failure is a degeneracy of the structure its axis tracks — the budget's sub-capacity values, the induced action on the witness classes — and neither entails the other.

*Proof.* The two failure conditions live on independent data: the upper substrate's budget structure $(\Phi_S, C_S)$ (Theorem 1) and the induced map $\phi_*$ on the lower carrier's witness classes (Proposition 4.3). These vary independently — nestability turns only on whether the budget admits a sub-capacity motion, witness preservation only on whether $\phi_*$ keeps the witness-bearing classes out of the pairing kernel: a substrate with abundant sub-capacity budget can still host a witness-annihilating transport on its lower carrier, and budget saturation says nothing about any transport's induced map. The independence is exhibited constructively *as independent-axis data*: explicit finite instances realise every budget verdict (a subcritical self-loop; a sealed core) and every witness verdict (identity, degree-zero, and mixed figure-eight transports of a carrier), in all six pairings. On the nestable side the two verdicts co-realise within one nested instance; a sealed core, hosting no nested pair, carries no transport verdict *of its own* — for those rows the pairing is of independent data, which is precisely the point: neither axis constrains the other. $\square$

**Proposition 5.2 (The bridge couples the axes).** Theorem 2 (Spin as Maintenance Geometry) is the structural reason the two axes are not independent in a boundary-layer-admissible pair. The single spin one-form $\omega_{S_i}$ carries the witness class $[\eta_{S_i}]$ (the witness-axis datum of Theorem 3) *and*, via the boundary integral of its declared envelope realisation, the maintenance cost $M^{\mathrm{cost}}_{S_i} = c \cdot |\sigma^{(M)}_{S_i}|$ (the budget-axis datum entering the X+M ledger of Theorem 2). One field, two ledger projections. On the boundary-witness-compatible subclass (Definition 3.5) the coupling sharpens to an identity — the two projections coincide numerically, $\sigma^{(M)}_{S_i} = \langle [\eta_{S_i}], [\gamma_i] \rangle$ (Proposition 3.7).

*Proof.* By Proposition 3.4 the witness pairing and the maintenance-circulation are two pairings of the same spin field $\omega_{S_i}$ — over the recurrence cycles on the lower carrier (witness) and over the boundary cycle in the upper neighbourhood (maintenance), distinct loci per Remark 3.3a. The first feeds the witness axis (Regime W is $[\eta_{S_i}] = 0$); the second feeds the budget axis (the maintenance overhead in the X+M law). Thus the same field is read on both axes, and the bridge of Theorem 2 is the link between them. $\square$

**Definition 5.3 (Declared source-localisation channel).** For a nested pair under sealed declarations (XIV companion Definition 2.6), a *source-localisation channel* is a declared, sealed triple: a finite *source alphabet* $Z$ with $|Z| \ge 2$ — the declared set of upper-source hypotheses for an event (which upper action, locus, or drive produced it); a rational prior $p(z) > 0$ on $Z$; and a finite *inward observation datum* $W_i$ with a rational channel matrix $P(w \mid z)$ — $W_i$ a declared function of the lower substrate's *own* data (its trajectory, its burden increments, its declared readings). $W_i$ is the mirror-direction counterpart of the declared observation channel $Y_S$ of XIV companion Definition 2.6: $Y_S$ structures what the substrate *exposes upward* across the layer boundary; $W_i$ structures what the lower can *read inward* about the upper source — two distinct declared data, not one channel used twice. All three components are sealed before any localisation is scored.

**Proposition 5.4 (Source-localisation floor).** Let $(Z, p, P)$ be a declared source-localisation channel, and let $\hat{z} = g(W_i)$ be *any* estimator of the source from the lower's observation. Then the error probability $P_e := \Pr[\hat{z} \neq Z]$ satisfies
$$ P_e \;\ge\; \frac{H(Z) - I(Z; W_i) - \log 2}{\log |Z|}, $$
with entropies in one fixed base; for the uniform prior the numerator reads $\log|Z| - I(Z; W_i) - \log 2$. Consequently, under a declared *mutual-information bound* $I(Z; W_i) \le \beta$ — the mirror-direction analogue of the HF-NR bound's declared $\varepsilon$-budget — every estimator errs with probability at least $\delta$ whenever $\beta \le H(Z) - \log 2 - \delta \log |Z|$.

*Proof.* Fano's inequality on the finite pair $(Z, \hat{z})$: with $E$ the error indicator, $H(Z \mid W_i) \le H(Z \mid \hat{z}) \le H(E) + P_e \log(|Z| - 1) \le \log 2 + P_e \log |Z|$ — conditioning on the estimator's output cannot lower the conditional entropy below conditioning on the data it is a function of; the error indicator's entropy is at most $\log 2$; given a correct guess $Z$ is determined, and given an error it ranges over at most $|Z| - 1$ letters. Since $H(Z \mid W_i) = H(Z) - I(Z; W_i)$, rearranging gives the display. $\blacksquare$

On the register: the inequality is *derived* — finite-alphabet information theory, self-contained above; what is *declared* is the channel itself ($Z$, $p$, $P$) and any mutual-information bound placed on it, in exactly the register of the NR-$\varepsilon$ budget it mirrors. The floor's arithmetic is exercised by the bundle harness (`fano_channel.py`): the exact best-estimator (MAP) error sits on or above the floor across declared channels, and the floor rises as the channel degrades. Certifying that a declared matrix faithfully represents a physical observation is deployment-side and open (Open Problem 7.8).

**[ALIGNMENT]** *Relation to HF-NR.* The corpus already carries one information-theoretic bound at this boundary: the holding-field non-reconstructibility bound HF-NR (ONTOΣ XI §2.3; applied as XIV §6's epistemic-independence discriminator), $I(H_{S_i}; Y_S) \le \varepsilon_T$ — the *upper* observer cannot reconstruct the lower's internal structure from the lower's declared emission. Proposition 5.4 bounds the *opposite* direction: the *lower* observer cannot localise the upper source from its declared inward observation. The two bounds are independent declarations over distinct channels ($Y_S$ outward, $W_i$ inward); together they close a two-sided epistemic firewall around the layer boundary, and the reconstruction-vs-detection distinction of the *Identifiability Bridge* §1.1 applies on both sides — detection *that* something acts is bounded but not annihilated (any property statistic inherits the leakage budget by data processing, without being forced to zero); reconstruction and localisation of *what* acts is what the two bounds bite on.

**Remark 5.5 (The imposition certificate).** Two declarations combine into a derived certificate. Let a deployment declare an $(a, \varepsilon)$-drift class (Definition 2.9) with $\lceil 1/a \rceil \cdot \varepsilon < 1$, and a source-localisation channel with $I(Z; W_i) \le \beta$ where $\beta \le H(Z) - \log 2 - \delta \log |Z|$. Then, for every in-class trajectory extending over at least $\lceil 1/a \rceil$ windows, the lower substrate is forced to first crossing within $\lceil 1/a \rceil$ windows at image burden strictly below the upper capacity (Proposition 2.10) — *and* no estimator available to the lower localises the source of what is happening to it with error below $\delta$ (Proposition 5.4). Strong structural effect at bounded upper cost, with source localisation bounded away from certainty: the quantitative form of the epistemic-independence thesis the corpus states categorically (XIV.2's cross-layer derivative is indifferent to the lower's epistemic localisation of the source) — the effect registers; the source does not resolve. The certificate asserts nothing beyond the conjunction of its two declared hypotheses and their two derived consequences.

The three statements assemble into the following reading of the eligibility level of XIV's death map.

## 6. Scope and Class-Conditionality

The class-conditional status of the four theorems, in the discipline of XIV companion §5.

- **Theorem 1 (Nestability Criterion)** holds *unconditionally in the finite-state substrate category $\mathsf{Sub}$*: the construction of the inner substrate carried by a subcritical trajectory and the reverse extraction of a subcritical trajectory from an IE witness are both finite combinatorial. The criterion's content is a *finite-capacity* phenomenon: at $C_S = \infty$ it reduces to the near-trivial "some non-empty trajectory has finite burden" (any finite-burden motion is then subcritical), so the sealed-core / sub-capacity reading presumes $C_S < \infty$. The continuous (measurable / topological) case requires the certificate-required interface of [admissibility] Part III for the trajectory-carrier construction and is Open Problem 7.1. The nestability margin (Proposition 2.4) quantifies this criterion within the same finite-capacity scope, adding a perturbation-robustness certificate; the margin also bounds the pair-level IE-robustness of every nesting over the substrate (Proposition 2.7), and both the criterion and the margin are computable in one scan of the admissible edges (Remark 2.8). The drift layer (Definition 2.9, Proposition 2.10) holds under its declared window discipline: the forced-crossing horizon and the image-cost bound are finite arithmetic on the declared bands, clause (iii) requires $\lceil 1/a \rceil \cdot \varepsilon < 1$ and is withheld otherwise, and a trajectory measured outside its bands falsifies the class membership, not the proposition. The input is a nested-pair candidate; genuine nesting is the *output* of clause (iii), not a hypothesis.
- **Theorem 2 (Spin as Maintenance Geometry)** holds *in the boundary-layer-admissible class* (Definition 3.2): a declared homologically non-trivial oriented $1$-cycle $\Gamma_{\nu, e}$ and a declared envelope realisation $\widetilde{\omega}^{\nu, e}_{S_i}$ of the lower spin form on $U_{\nu, e}$ (closed there for the net-circulation reading; a per-functional requirement, Definition 3.1) must be supplied for $\sigma^{(M)}$ to be well-defined and typed (Lemma 3.3, Definition 3.2). The proportionality is a *declared* structural law of the class, in the register of the X+M law of XIV companion Theorem 5 / Definition 9.1 — the declared boundary functional $\mathcal{N}$ (net circulation / norm / flux), the linear form, and the constant $c$ are declared, not derived — and $\sigma^{(M)}$ is read in the upper-state-space neighbourhood, distinct in locus from the recurrence witness $[\eta_{S_i}]$ (Remark 3.3a). On the *boundary-witness-compatible* subclass (Definition 3.5, net-circulation mode) the net-circulation $\sigma^{(M)}$ is not free but equals the transported witness period $\langle [\eta_{S_i}], [\gamma_i] \rangle$ (Proposition 3.7); the proportionality $M^{\mathrm{cost}} = c \cdot |\sigma^{(M)}|$ stays declared there too. Outside the class — non-smooth, fractal, or absent boundary — it retains the structural-statement-under-operational-test register. Non-smooth boundaries are Open Problem 7.3; calibration of $c$ is Open Problem 7.2. The empirical content of the declared proportionality is the multi-event $c$-consistency test (Proposition 3.8): a single positive-circulation event is always fittable by an unsealed $c$, so the falsifiable claim is that one shared sealed $c$ fits a class of $\ge 2$ events within tolerance (a single event can already violate an *already-sealed* $c$, clause (a)). On the compatible subclass with integral witness data (normalization fixing only the unit reading) the test sharpens to the quantised M-only lattice falsifier with its positive floor (Proposition 3.11, $\varepsilon < c/2$); the two-channel refinement carries its own class-level falsifier — the exact two-parameter feasibility test of Proposition 3.15; the channel inequality (Lemma 3.12) and the harmonic $L^2$-minimality (Proposition 3.13) are unconditional statements of the declared loci's linear algebra, holding wherever the data are typed. The minimal-entrainment bound (Proposition 3.14) is of the same unconditional kind: it derives the floor of the norm and quadratic readings (not of the flux reading — Lemma 3.12's exception stands) — and, under a named effort reading, the form of the minimal cost — from the locus geometry, leaving the Bridge Law's status for general deployments unchanged.
- **The tower layer (§4, Definitions 4.8 and 4.10, Lemma 4.9, Propositions 4.11 and 4.13)** is typed, not speculative: Lemma 4.9 is set-theoretic (injectivity of the nesting morphisms); Proposition 4.11 combines finite exhibition with Proposition 4.3 and holds wherever its two typing conditions — image-preservation, and carrier-coherence — hold; carrier-coherence is *derived* for $\mathsf{AdmDyn}$-restrictions (the closed-walk argument of Definition 4.10, under its named recurrence-edge reading) and *declared* for general transports; Proposition 4.13(i) is Proposition 3.7 per level, while 4.13(ii)'s composition and the witness-alignment it needs are declarations, named as such. Remark 4.12 is a sum of independent per-level bounds.
- **Theorem 3 (Core-Reduction)**, and its general form **Proposition 4.3 (Witness Transport Kernel Criterion)**, hold *unconditionally on $\mathsf{TopWit}$*: they are general topology (contractible carriers have trivial $H^1$; the pairing $\langle [\eta'], \phi_*[\gamma] \rangle$ is well-defined for any induced $\phi_*$) applied to the imported carrier category, requiring no class restriction beyond the existence of the identity-supporting carrier (XIV companion Definition 11.1). **Theorem 4 (Core-Reduction Executes Regime W under Preservation Conditions)** delivers Regime W condition (4) unconditionally (via Theorem 3 — the only condition established formally); the *full* Regime-W classification — conditions (1)–(3) as well — follows, for *budget- and interior-preserving* core-reductions, from the imported prose / deployment-witness criteria of XIV companion Definition 11.4 clause (3), witnessed by the folding construction of XIV companion Theorem 10, not re-proved here. A core-reduction that also closes the interior or spends the budget delivers (4) but need not be in Regime W.
- **The localisation layer (§5: Definition 5.3, Proposition 5.4, Remark 5.5)** holds relative to its declaration and nowhere else: Proposition 5.4 is standard finite-alphabet information theory over the declared channel $(Z, p, P)$, and its floor binds every estimator *of that channel* — certifying that the declared matrix faithfully represents the physical observation is deployment-side (Open Problem 7.8). Remark 5.5's certificate asserts exactly the conjunction of its two declared hypotheses and their derived consequences, nothing further.

The empirical content of all of the above lives in the falsification surface of the essay (ONTOΣ XV §6): the enumeration of $\Phi_S$ values against the half-open range $[0, C_S)$ (strictly $(0, C_S)$ for positive burdens) for Theorem 1; the window-band audit of the drift class (Definition 2.9 — an out-of-band window falsifies the membership declaration); the multi-event graph-Hodge boundary-circulation readings against one sealed $c$ (Proposition 3.8) for Theorem 2; the channel-declaration audit of the localisation floor (a measured localisation beating the declared floor falsifies the channel declaration); the pre/post graph-Hodge winding measurement under a synthetic folding for Theorems 3–4. The sealed-declaration discipline of XIV companion Definition 2.6 governs each test.

## 7. Open Problems

The deferred residue, retained as forward directions in the discipline of XIV companion §6.

- **OP 7.1 — Nestability for continuous substrates.** Extend Theorem 1 beyond finite-state $\mathsf{Sub}$ to measure-theoretic admissibility: the trajectory-carrier construction of the $(\Leftarrow)$ direction and the non-emptiness of the IE witness in the $(\Rightarrow)$ direction must be re-established under the certificate-required interface of [admissibility] Part III.
- **OP 7.2 — Calibration of $c$.** Theorem 2's maintenance-per-circulation-unit constant $c$ is declared at deployment. A per-domain measurement protocol relating $c$ to instrumented maintenance work (analogous to the saturation-witness discipline of XIV companion Remark 2.7) is open. The calibration protocol should also test the sealed $c$ against declared rival ledgers (length-, edge-count-, upper-cost-proportional) on holdout events, so that the surviving proportionality is not an artifact of a generic one-parameter fit. Under the named effort readings the floor constants are computed, not calibrated (Proposition 3.14); calibration concerns where deployments sit above the floor. A proposed table-top substrate-ladder protocol (the tariff curve $c(\mu)$ across declared substrates) and a contact-rig candidate ship in the bundle's protocol companion (*Two Funnels, One Spin*); the calibration itself remains open.
- **OP 7.3 — Non-smooth boundary envelopes.** Theorem 2 requires a boundary cycle supporting the line integral of the declared envelope realisation $\widetilde{\omega}^{\nu, e}_{S_i}$. The integration theory of such realisations over non-smooth or fractal boundaries $\partial \nu(X_i)$ — where the line integral is not classically defined — is open; the falsifier of essay §6 applies to finite-resolution approximations in the interim.
- **OP 7.4 — The depth-$n$ tower programme (residue).** The tower layer of §4 now supplies the inter-level transport typing (image-preservation, the derived restriction, carrier-coherence), level independence with the pinch criterion, additive floors, and the period ladder. The open residue of the original recursive-identity-transport direction (XIV companion §5.5 V2 note): the composite cohomology stack and a recursive transport functor; the *undeclared* cross-level rank bound (a pigeonhole confining how many levels' witnesses survive one composite event through a common bottleneck); and collapse propagation beyond the pinch criterion.
- **OP 7.5 — Bidirectional / feedback towers.** XV's nesting is one-directional (outer carries inner). Towers in which the inner substrate influences the outer require a distinct morphism structure, separate from $\mathsf{NestedSub}_{\mathrm{nested}}$ in the manner of XIV companion Remark 3.7.
- **OP 7.6 — Numerical / continuous-domain pilot.** A continuous-domain demonstrator of the maintenance-circulation reading (Theorem 2), analogous to the walker–swarm biomechanical pilot of XIV companion §11.3, running the graph-Hodge boundary instrument on a parameterised boundary envelope with positive and negative controls; the derived floor of Proposition 3.14 supplies its mandatory sanity gate. A synthetic continuous demonstrator ships with the bundle harness (`pilot_entrainment.py`): field-side and dynamics-side measurements through independent code paths, the derived-floor gate, and the sealed-$c$ holdout discipline with corruption, zero-spin, and cancellation controls — instrument-and-discipline validation in a controlled world; the physical-domain pilot remains open. A proposed table-top realisation (the four-run two-funnels protocol, with channel controls and the quasi-static kill-the-core comparison) ships in the bundle's protocol companion; the pilot itself remains open.
- **OP 7.7 — Boundary-witness-compatible verification and general-class loci-identification.** Proposition 3.7 relates the maintenance-circulation to the witness period *on a declared subclass* via a sealed map $\rho$ (H-a, H-b). The *algebraic* verification of H-a / H-b for declared finite-graph data is mechanical (a finite computation on the induced maps) and is not the open part. Three residues remain open: (i) a deployment-side protocol certifying that a declared $\rho$ *faithfully represents the physical embedding* — that the graph map handed to the algebraic check is the right abstraction of the deployment, and its continuous / non-graph counterpart; (ii) whether any relation between the upper-envelope and lower-carrier loci holds for the *general* boundary-layer-admissible class, beyond the compatible subclass — the loci-identification question Remark 3.3a leaves open outside §3.5; and (iii) the analogous certification for the sealed realisation rule of Definition 3.2 — that the class-level operator carrying $\omega_{S_i}$ to the envelope faithfully represents the physical transfer — together with its formalisation as a per-event family of operators $R_{\nu, e} \colon \Omega^1(M^{\mathrm{carrier}}_{S_i}) \to \Omega^1(U_{\nu, e})$ induced by one rule fixed for the class (naturality in $e$ being the condition Definition 3.2's prose clause describes).
- **OP 7.8 — Dynamic and stochastic extensions of the drift and localisation layers.** The drift layer (Definition 2.9, Proposition 2.10) is deterministic and finite-state: continuous-time and stochastic drift classes (supermartingale bands in the Foster–Lyapunov style), together with the full storage-function typing of the maintenance ledger in the dissipativity vocabulary that §2's alignment note names but does not construct, are open. The localisation layer (Definition 5.3, Proposition 5.4) is finite-alphabet over a declared channel: continuous observation alphabets, estimator families with side information, and the deployment-side protocol certifying that a declared channel matrix faithfully represents the physical observation (the analogue of OP 7.7's $\rho$-certification) are open. The deployment-side measurement protocol for the window bands themselves — what physical instrumentation counts as one window — belongs here as well.

## 9. Glossary

- **Subcritical admissible trajectory.** An admissible trajectory traversing at least one edge with cumulative burden strictly below capacity (Definition 2.1). The existence of one in $\mathfrak{S}$ is equivalent to $\mathfrak{S}$'s nestability (Theorem 1).
- **Sealed core.** A substrate of positive size whose every edge-traversing motion saturates the full capacity, leaving no subcritical trajectory; the canonical non-nestable witness (Counter-example 2.2).
- **Maintenance-circulation $\sigma^{(M)}_{S_i}(\nu, e)$.** The value $\mathcal{N}_{\Gamma_{\nu, e}}(\widetilde{\omega}^{\nu, e}_{S_i})$ of a declared boundary functional (net circulation / norm / vorticity-flux) on the *declared envelope realisation* $\widetilde{\omega}^{\nu, e}_{S_i}$ of the spin field over a declared oriented $1$-cycle $\Gamma_{\nu, e}$ (Definition 3.1); event-indexed; for the net-circulation choice a period (Lemma 3.3). Read in the upper-state-space neighbourhood — a distinct locus from the recurrence witness $[\eta_{S_i}]$ (Remark 3.3a). The name follows the canonical net-circulation choice; under the norm / flux functionals the value is a boundary maintenance reading, not a circulation in the strict sense (Remark 3.10's $\sigma_H$ / $\Lambda_{\mathrm{loc}}$ separate the channels).
- **Envelope realisation $\widetilde{\omega}^{\nu, e}_{S_i}$.** A declared (sealed) extension of the lower spin one-form $\omega_{S_i} = S_i^\flat$ to an integrable one-form on a neighbourhood of the declared cycle $\Gamma_{\nu, e}$ in the upper state space $X$ (Definition 3.2), closedness a *per-functional* requirement (closed near $\Gamma$ for net circulation, unused for the norm, excluded on $\Sigma$ for the vorticity-flux); the datum that types $\sigma^{(M)}$ (a lower-carrier form is not integrable against an upper-space cycle without it). Declared as one sealed realisation rule for the class, applied per event through the embedding geometry — never re-declared event by event (Definition 3.2).
- **Boundary-layer-admissible class.** Nested pairs whose embedding envelope supports a homologically non-trivial ($[\Gamma] \neq 0$) cycle around which the declared envelope realisation is integrable, closedness a per-functional requirement (Definitions 3.1–3.2); the scope of Theorem 2.
- **Maintenance-per-circulation-unit constant $c$.** The declared, sealed proportionality of Theorem 2; $M^{\mathrm{cost}} = c \cdot |\sigma^{(M)}|$. Calibration is Open Problem 7.2.
- **$M^{\mathrm{carrier}}_{S_i}$ / $M^{\mathrm{cost}}_{S_i}(\nu, e)$.** The identity-supporting carrier (topological) versus the maintenance-of-nesting cost (scalar); the XIV companion §11.1 disambiguation, imported.
- **TopWit-honest transport.** A carrier-map that is witness-preserving (preserves non-vanishing of the witness pairing) *and* does not factor through a contractible carrier (Definition 4.1); the honest transport unit is the pair $(M^{\mathrm{carrier}}, [\eta])$ together.
- **Core-reduction.** A transport whose carrier-map factors through a contractible space and whose post-event carrier is that core itself (or deformation-retracts to it); the factoring alone already induces the zero map on $H^1$, so no non-trivial witness is carried across (Theorem 3(i)), and the contractible post-carrier annihilates the witness outright (Theorem 3(ii)), executing Regime W by transport (Theorem 4). It is the *contractible instance* of the Witness Transport Kernel Criterion (Proposition 4.3).
- **Witness Transport Kernel Criterion.** The general classification of a transport by whether its induced $\phi_*$ sends each witness-bearing recurrence class into the pairing kernel of $[\eta']$: witness-preserving / annihilating / partial (Proposition 4.3). Core-reduction (Theorem 3) is the canonical — not the only — annihilating case; a degree-zero map of a non-contractible carrier annihilates too.
- **Nestability margin $\mu_{\mathrm{nest}}$.** $1 - \min\{\Phi_S(\gamma)/C_S : \gamma \in \mathrm{Adm}_S,\ |\gamma| \ge 1\}$ for finite $C_S$ and a substrate with some non-empty admissible motion (Proposition 2.4); $> 0$ iff nestable (Theorem 1), and $\ge \delta$ certifies that per-edge burden shifts $< \delta C_S$ preserve nestability.
- **Multi-event $c$-consistency.** The empirical-mode falsifier of the Bridge Law (Proposition 3.8, norm mode): the declared proportionality $M^{\mathrm{cost}} = c\,|\sigma^{(M)}|$ is falsified for a class iff no single shared $c > 0$ fits $\ge 2$ measured events within tolerance (a single positive-circulation event is always fittable by an unsealed $c$; a norm-mode event with $|\sigma| = 0$ and $M > \varepsilon$ falsifies outright).
- **Autonomous nested embedding.** A nested embedding whose inner declaration — including its spin/witness data — is sealed before the witnessing event, whose budget is not extracted from the witnessing trajectory, and whose carrier bears a witness class (Definition 2.5); strictly stronger than formal IE-nestability (Proposition 2.6), with deployment-audited hostability as the further, deployment-level rung.
- **Primitive-normalized witness.** A witness *class* under a declared, sealed unit choice giving period $\pm 1$ on a declared primitive recurrence cycle (Definition 3.9); on the boundary-witness-compatible subclass (net-circulation mode) this fixes the rescaling freedom so the Bridge constant $c$ reads as maintenance per witness-unit.
- **Two-channel refinement.** The jointly-declared law $M^{\mathrm{cost}} = c_H |\sigma_H| + c_L \Lambda_{\mathrm{loc}}$ splitting the boundary reading into the gauge-invariant homological channel and the local channel (norm: gauge-sensitive; flux: gauge-invariant but class-blind) — canonical pairing net-circulation with norm (Remark 3.10). Its Regime-W signature is read at the carrier level: the transported witness pairing dies while the carrier field's magnitude reading may survive.
- **Two-channel feasibility.** The class-level falsifier of the two-channel law (Proposition 3.15): a sealed pair $(c_H, c_L) \ge 0$ exists iff the bands' *joint intersection* meets the closed non-negative quadrant ($F \neq \emptyset$) — decided exactly by two-variable elimination. Two independent-reading events falsify only through positivity; genuine overdetermination begins at three; with $\Lambda \equiv 0$ the verdict reduces to the single-channel discipline (Proposition 3.8) under clause (d)'s agreement condition.
- **Witness retention index $R_\phi$.** The fraction of a declared finite set $W_0$ of witness-bearing recurrence classes whose transported pairing survives $\phi$ (Remark 4.4); refines the preserving / annihilating / partial trichotomy into a gradient relative to $W_0$.
- **Maintenance quantum.** On the compatible subclass with *integral* witness data (primitive normalization fixes only the unit reading), $\sigma^{(M)} \in \mathbb{Z} \setminus \{0\}$, so $M^{\mathrm{cost}} = c\,|\sigma^{(M)}| \in \{c, 2c, \dots\}$ with the positive floor $M^{\mathrm{cost}} \ge c$ (Proposition 3.11): no witness-bearing compatible nesting is maintenance-free, and measured costs must sit on the $c$-lattice (the M-only falsifier; lattice discrimination for $\varepsilon < c/2$, the floor clause for any $\varepsilon < c$).
- **Channel inequality.** $\Lambda_{\mathrm{loc}} \ge |\sigma_H|$ on the declared locus under the counting measure — and any measure datum $w \ge 1$ — for the *norm* reading of the local channel (Lemma 3.12; the flux reading is not bounded below by the period, and an arbitrary declared measure with $w < 1$ can break the display — the measure-general floor is Proposition 3.14's $c_1 \cdot |\sigma|$ with $c_1 = \min_e w(e)$): a live homological channel forces a live norm channel; the converse fails (alternating realisation: period $0$, norm $4$).
- **Witness capacity / bottleneck bound.** The number of declared witness-bearing classes with linearly independent transported images is at most $\mathrm{rank}\,\phi_* \le \min b_1$ over every carrier in the chain (Proposition 4.6); Theorem 3 is the $b_1 = 0$ case.
- **Class-retention $R^{\mathrm{cls}}_\phi$.** The fraction of $W_0$ with $\phi_*[\gamma] \neq 0$; monotone non-increasing along chains, unlike the witness-relative pairing retention (Proposition 4.7).
- **Harmonic representative.** The unique divergence-free representative of a realisation's class; it keeps all periods and uniquely minimises the weighted $L^2$-magnitude (Proposition 3.13) — the cheapest global field-magnitude compatible with a fixed homological reading (not, in general, the minimiser of the $\ell^1$ norm functional).
- **Minimal-entrainment bound.** On a declared locus, the derived variational floor of the norm and quadratic readings (not of the flux reading) over all realisations carrying a fixed period: $c_1 |\sigma|$ for the weighted norm reading ($c_1 = \min_e w(e)$), $\sigma^2 / L_w$ for the quadratic reading, attained by the concentrated and $w$-harmonic profiles respectively (Proposition 3.14). Under a named effort reading of the local channel it computes the form and constants of the minimal maintenance cost; the Bridge Law for general deployments remains declared.
- **Tower transport; image-preservation; derived restriction.** A transport at level $k$ of a tower is image-preserving at level $k-1$ when it maps the embedded lower image into the post-tower embedded image (Definition 4.8); the lower transport is then *derived* — $(\nu')^{-1} \circ \phi \circ \nu$, total and unique — rather than separately declared (Lemma 4.9).
- **Carrier-coherence.** The condition that the derived lower transport induces a graph map on the lower's own recurrence carrier (Definition 4.10): automatic for $\mathsf{AdmDyn}$-restrictions, declared for general (folding-type) transports.
- **Pinch criterion.** The sufficient condition for killing a lower level: the derived restriction sends *every* witness-bearing recurrence class of that level into the pairing kernel of the transported witness — per class, each class so sent is annihilated (Proposition 4.11(b) — Proposition 4.3 read through the derived restriction). Level death otherwise propagates in neither direction by itself (Proposition 4.11(a): all four verdict combinations realisable).
- **Period ladder.** Per-pair equalities $\sigma_k = \langle [\eta_{(k-1)}], [\gamma_{(k-1)}] \rangle$ on compatible pairs (parallel, one per level — Proposition 4.13(i)); they compose into a top-reads-bottom chain under a *declared* witness-alignment at each intermediate level (Proposition 4.13(ii); no composition is claimed otherwise).
- **Drift class $(a, \varepsilon)$.** A declared, sealed window rule with per-window bands: every lower window's prefix-difference burden increment $\ge a \cdot C_{S_i}$, every image window's $\le \varepsilon \cdot C_S$ (Definition 2.9). Membership is measured against the declared bands; an out-of-band window falsifies the membership declaration, not a theorem.
- **Forced Independent-Exhaustion.** On an $(a, \varepsilon)$-drift-class trajectory with $\lceil 1/a \rceil \cdot \varepsilon < 1$, first crossing arrives within $\lceil 1/a \rceil$ windows with the pushforward strictly subcritical — the IE witness is forced by the declared class, not exhibited by example, and membership of the genuinely-nested subcategory is thereby *derived* for the candidate pair (Proposition 2.10); at $\lceil 1/a \rceil \cdot \varepsilon \ge 1$ the subcriticality clause is withheld.
- **Source-localisation channel / floor.** A declared, sealed finite channel $(Z, p, P(w \mid z))$ from upper-source hypotheses to the lower's inward observation datum $W_i$ (Definition 5.3; the mirror direction of $Y_S$); every estimator of $Z$ from $W_i$ errs with probability $\ge (H(Z) - I(Z; W_i) - \log 2)/\log|Z|$ (Proposition 5.4).
- **Imposition certificate.** The derived conjunction of the drift and localisation layers (Remark 5.5): forced first crossing at image cost strictly below the upper capacity, with every lower-side source estimator's error bounded below — effect without localisation, the quantitative form of the epistemic-independence thesis.
